# What's Wrong with Vegetables? Which Ones Are the Worst?

**Categories:** Vegetables, Gut Health

## TLDR

Paul Saladino argues that most vegetables contain defense chemicals — lectins, oxalates, isothiocyanates, goitrogens, and other compounds — that plants produce to deter animals from eating them. He considers leafy greens, cruciferous vegetables, and nightshades the most problematic, and recommends replacing them with organ meats and low-toxin fruit for superior nutrition without the plant defense chemicals.

## Background

The idea that vegetables are universally healthy is one of the most deeply held beliefs in nutrition. Saladino challenges this directly, drawing on evolutionary biology: unlike fruit (which plants produce to attract animals for seed dispersal), leaves, roots, stems, and seeds are parts the plant needs to survive — so they are defended with chemical warfare. His position does not claim vegetables are acutely toxic, but that chronic exposure to their defense chemicals contributes to gut damage, nutrient malabsorption, autoimmune conditions, and inflammation over time.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**Core principle:** Plants produce defense chemicals in their leaves, roots, stems, and seeds to deter herbivory. Fruit is the exception — the plant wants its fruit eaten to spread seeds, so fruit contains the fewest defensive compounds.

**The worst vegetables (highest in defense chemicals):**

- **Nightshades** (tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, white potatoes, goji berries) — contain lectins, solanine, chaconine, and glycoalkaloids. Associated with autoimmune flares. Saladino reports personal brain fog from white potatoes. Despite being botanical fruits, nightshades are excluded from the diet.
- **Leafy greens high in oxalates** (spinach, kale, chard, beets) — oxalates bind calcium, contribute to kidney stones, and may accumulate in tissues over time.
- **Cruciferous vegetables** (broccoli, kale, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts) — produce isothiocyanates when chewed, which are sulfur-containing compounds that can impair thyroid function (goitrogens) and irritate the gut.
- **Alliums** (garlic, onions) — contain sulfur compounds that can irritate the digestive tract.

**Specific plant defense chemicals he discusses:**

| Chemical | Found In | Mechanism of Harm |
|---|---|---|
| Lectins | Nightshades, grains, legumes | Damage gut lining, associated with autoimmune issues |
| Oxalates | Spinach, kale, chard, beets, turmeric | Bind calcium, form kidney stones, accumulate in tissues |
| Isothiocyanates | Cruciferous vegetables | Impair thyroid function, irritate gut |
| Solanine/chaconine | Potatoes, nightshades | Neurotoxic at high doses, cause brain fog and inflammation |
| Phytates | Grains, legumes, nuts, seeds | Bind minerals (zinc, iron, calcium), preventing absorption |
| Saponins | Quinoa, legumes | Damage gut lining, increase permeability |
| Gluten | Wheat, barley, rye | Triggers zonulin release, opens gut tight junctions in everyone |
| Goitrogens | Cruciferous vegetables, soy | Interfere with thyroid hormone production |
| Capsaicin | Hot peppers | Directly damages gut epithelial cells |

**What he considers more tolerable (if any vegetables are eaten):**
- Squash flesh (no skin or seeds) — low-toxin, 200g/day in his own plan
- Cucumber flesh (no skin) — minimal defense chemicals in the flesh
- Avocado — technically a fruit, low in plant toxins
- Sweet potato — tolerated by some, though squash is preferred
- White rice — "the least bad grain" but still avoided due to arsenic; some tolerate it

**Recommended alternatives to vegetables for nutrients:**

Saladino argues organ meats are "nature's multivitamin" and provide superior bioavailability of every nutrient attributed to vegetables, plus nutrients plants don't contain at all:
- Vitamin A (retinol): liver, not beta-carotene from carrots
- Vitamin C: fresh fruit and organ meats
- Iron (heme): red meat, spleen — far more bioavailable than plant non-heme iron
- Zinc: red meat, liver
- Folate: liver
- B12: all animal foods (absent from plants entirely)
- Vitamin K2: liver, raw dairy, egg yolks (not found in plant foods)

## Key Points

- Plants defend their leaves, roots, stems, and seeds with chemical compounds. Fruit is the exception — it's designed to be eaten.
- "Eat your vegetables" is a cultural assumption, not an evolutionary requirement. Humans thrived for hundreds of thousands of years without cultivated vegetables.
- Organ meats provide every nutrient attributed to vegetables in more bioavailable forms, plus nutrients not found in plants at all (B12, K2, heme iron, creatine, carnitine, choline).
- Fiber from vegetables is not necessary for gut health. A 2012 RCT (Ho et al.) showed 100% constipation resolution on zero fiber. Fermented dairy and bone broth support the gut without plant toxin exposure.
- The Hadza, often cited as evidence for high-fiber diets, actually spit out most fiber from tubers after chewing. Their gut microbiome diversity comes from outdoor living, not fiber intake.
- Polyphenols and antioxidants from vegetables are reframed as plant defense chemicals that trigger mild stress responses (hormesis). The body's endogenous antioxidant systems, supported by animal nutrients like selenium and zinc, are more effective.

## Common Mistakes

- **Assuming all plant foods are equal.** Fruit (designed to be eaten) is fundamentally different from leaves, roots, and seeds (defended by toxins). The animal-based diet includes fruit but excludes most vegetables.
- **Thinking you need vegetables for fiber.** Fiber is not essential for gut health and may actually irritate an already damaged gut. Fermented foods and bone broth are better for the microbiome.
- **Believing beta-carotene equals vitamin A.** Conversion from beta-carotene to retinol is inefficient (as low as 3–6% in some people). Liver provides preformed retinol directly.
- **Eating vegetables "for the microbiome."** A Stanford study (Wastyk et al., 2021) found fermented foods outperformed high-fiber diets for microbiome diversity.
- **Keeping nightshades while removing other "bad" foods.** Nightshades are among the most problematic plant foods for autoimmune conditions but are often overlooked because tomatoes and peppers seem benign.
- **Not removing skins and seeds from tolerated plant foods.** If eating squash or cucumber, always peel and deseed — defense chemicals concentrate in the skin and seeds.
