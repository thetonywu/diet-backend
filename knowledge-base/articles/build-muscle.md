# How to Build Muscle and Improve Fitness on the Animal-Based Diet

**Categories:** Fitness, Nutrients, Hormones

## TLDR

Paul Saladino's muscle-building framework prioritizes food quality over calorie counting: eat roughly 1 lb of grass-fed meat per 100 lbs of body weight, include organ meats daily, and consume 150–300+ grams of carbohydrates from fruit and honey to support hormones and recovery. He recommends resistance training over chronic endurance exercise, 5g creatine daily, and warns that strict low-carb diets sabotage muscle gains by suppressing testosterone and elevating cortisol.

## Background

Most conventional fitness nutrition revolves around protein powders, calorie tracking, and meal timing. Saladino takes a different approach: optimize the quality and nutrient density of food, support hormone production with adequate carbohydrates, and let body composition follow. His own experience on strict carnivore — where his testosterone dropped to ~300 ng/dL despite eating plenty of protein — informs his strong stance that carbohydrates from whole-food sources are essential for athletic performance and muscle growth.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Protein

- Aim for at least **1 lb of meat per 100 lbs of body weight daily** — roughly 1–1.2g protein per pound of goal body weight
- Preferred sources: grass-fed, grass-finished ruminant meats (beef, bison, lamb, goat, elk, venison)
- 80/20 ground beef is a practical and affordable daily staple
- Avoid relying on conventional chicken and pork — monogastric animals fed corn and soy accumulate high levels of linoleic acid in their fat
- Eat 2–3 meals per day in a ~10-hour eating window; he warns against OMAD (one meal a day) for active people as it suppresses testosterone and IGF-1

### Carbohydrates for performance

- **Minimum 150g/day** even for sedentary people; active individuals should eat significantly more
- Activity-based formula (grams per lb of goal body weight):
  - Low activity (0–3 hrs/week): 0.7–0.9g/lb
  - Moderate (3–6 hrs/week): 0.8–1.1g/lb
  - High (6–12 hrs/week): 1.0–1.5g/lb
  - Very high (12+ hrs/week): 1.2–1.7g/lb
- Sources: watermelon, blueberries, mango, papaya, oranges, bananas, raw honey, coconut water, fresh-squeezed orange juice
- Saladino personally eats ~300g carbs/day as an active surfer

### Organ meats

- **Liver** (0.5 oz/day or 2–3 oz/week): retinol, zinc, B12, copper — required for testosterone synthesis and energy
- **Heart**: CoQ10 for mitochondrial energy production and ATP synthesis
- **Kidney**: selenium and B vitamins for thyroid function, which regulates metabolic rate
- **Testicle**: activin, inhibin, selenium, zinc — direct testosterone support
- **Bone marrow**: fat-soluble nutrients and collagen for connective tissue

### Exercise philosophy

- **Resistance training and heavy lifting** are "one of the highest-leverage testosterone interventions" — he prioritizes this over cardio
- Avoid chronic endurance exercise, which may harm hormonal profiles
- His pre-workout protocol: raw milk + honey + 5g creatine + sea salt before 6 AM
- Daily physical activity is essential — at minimum, two or three 15-minute walks around meals for blood glucose control

## Key Points

- Protein quality matters as much as quantity — grass-fed ruminant meat has a superior fatty acid profile compared to grain-fed chicken or pork
- Insufficient carbohydrate intake "suppresses hormones, disrupts sleep, and causes muscle loss over time" — the opposite of what most low-carb advocates claim
- Creatine is Saladino's #1 recommended supplement; its synthesis consumes ~50% of the body's daily methylation capacity, so supplementing frees resources for neurotransmitter production and DNA repair
- Organ meats provide nutrients critical for athletic performance (CoQ10, heme iron, B12, carnitine) in their most bioavailable forms — he calls them "nature's multivitamin"
- Saladino opposes calorie counting — his framework is "fix the inputs and body composition follows"
- He went from ~300 ng/dL testosterone on strict carnivore to 857 ng/dL at age 48 after adding fruit and honey — demonstrating that carbohydrates directly support the hormonal environment needed for muscle growth

## Common Mistakes

- **Going strict carnivore or keto for body composition** — very low-carb diets chronically elevate SHBG and cortisol while suppressing free testosterone, undermining muscle growth
- **Relying on protein powders and multivitamins instead of whole food** — Saladino considers these synthetic supplements with industrial fillers unnecessary when eating properly
- **Eating OMAD or extreme fasting while training** — suppresses testosterone and IGF-1, both critical for muscle protein synthesis
- **Choosing chicken breast over beef for "lean protein"** — conventional chicken is high in linoleic acid and nutritionally inferior to ruminant meat
- **Neglecting organ meats** — missing CoQ10 from heart, retinol from liver, and selenium from kidney means missing key cofactors for energy and hormone production
- **Avoiding carbs around training** — fruit and honey before and after training support glycogen replenishment, cortisol management, and recovery
