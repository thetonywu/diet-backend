# What Does My Blood Work Mean on This Diet?

**Categories:** LDL / Cholesterol, Chronic Disease

## TLDR

Paul Saladino recommends a specific blood panel that prioritizes fasting insulin, TG/HDL ratio, and hsCRP over standard markers like total cholesterol. He uses much tighter optimal ranges than conventional medicine — fasting insulin below 5 (not 20), triglycerides below 70 (not 150), hsCRP below 1 (not 3). Elevated LDL is common on the animal-based diet but is clinically unimportant in his view when metabolic markers are favorable and a CAC scan shows zero plaque.

## Background

Many people transitioning to the animal-based diet see dramatic shifts in their blood work — triglycerides drop, HDL rises, but LDL often increases significantly. This alarms conventional doctors who focus on LDL as the primary risk marker. Saladino argues this framing is backwards: metabolic health markers matter far more than LDL, and the standard reference ranges used by most labs are dangerously loose, masking early dysfunction.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### The blood panel to request

Most doctors will not order all of these by default. Saladino recommends specifically requesting:

**Metabolic markers (most important):**
- Fasting insulin — his #1 marker; most labs don't include it unless asked
- Comprehensive metabolic panel with fasting glucose
- Hemoglobin A1c
- Lipid panel (triglycerides, HDL, LDL, total cholesterol)
- hsCRP (high-sensitivity C-reactive protein)
- Liver enzymes and GGT
- Uric acid
- Homocysteine

**Hormonal markers:**
- Total and free testosterone, SHBG
- Full thyroid panel (TSH, free T3, free T4)
- Cortisol, DHEA-S

**Imaging:**
- Coronary artery calcium (CAC) scan — he calls this the "gold standard" for actual plaque burden

### His optimal ranges vs standard ranges

| Marker | Saladino's Optimal | Standard "Normal" |
|---|---|---|
| Fasting insulin | < 5 μIU/mL (ideal < 3) | 2–20 μIU/mL |
| Fasting glucose | 70–85 mg/dL | 70–100 mg/dL |
| HbA1c | ≤ 5.3% | < 5.7% |
| Triglycerides | ≤ 70 mg/dL | < 150 mg/dL |
| HDL | ≥ 60 mg/dL | > 40 mg/dL |
| TG/HDL ratio | < 2 (optimal ~0.5) | Not routinely calculated |
| hsCRP | < 1 mg/L | < 3 mg/L |

### How to interpret your results

**Step 1:** Check metabolic health — fasting insulin < 5, triglycerides < 70, HDL > 60, TG/HDL < 2, hsCRP < 1.

**Step 2:** If those are favorable, elevated LDL is not a concern. Get a CAC scan to confirm zero plaque.

**Step 3:** If CAC is 0, do not accept a statin prescription regardless of LDL level.

### Common patterns on the animal-based diet

- Triglycerides fall to 40–70 mg/dL
- HDL rises to 70–90 mg/dL
- TG/HDL ratio drops well below 2
- Fasting insulin drops to 2–5 μIU/mL
- hsCRP drops below 1 mg/L
- LDL often rises to 200–500+ mg/dL, especially in lean, active individuals (the "Lean Mass Hyper-Responder" phenotype)

Saladino's own labs: LDL 533, CAC score 0, no arterial plaque on CT angiography — despite a family history of early heart disease.

## Key Points

- Fasting insulin is the most important metabolic marker but is rarely included in standard panels — you must specifically request it
- The TG/HDL ratio is Saladino's primary cardiovascular risk proxy; it reflects insulin sensitivity better than LDL
- Standard reference ranges are population-based, not health-based — a fasting insulin of 15 is "normal" but already indicates metabolic dysfunction
- The LMHR (Lean Mass Hyper-Responder) phenotype is common: lean, active people on animal-based diets show very high LDL alongside excellent metabolic markers and zero plaque
- The 2024 KETO trial (100 LMHR subjects, avg LDL 272) found no significant difference in coronary plaque vs. matched controls at LDL 123
- A CAC score of 0 in a metabolically healthy person is sufficient to deprioritize LDL concerns

## Common Mistakes

- **Panicking about elevated LDL without checking metabolic markers** — LDL alone is not meaningful; check insulin, triglycerides, HDL, and hsCRP first
- **Accepting standard reference ranges as optimal** — a fasting insulin of 15 is "in range" but already problematic
- **Not requesting fasting insulin** — most basic panels omit it; you must ask
- **Taking statins based on LDL alone** — Saladino recommends getting a CAC scan first; if it's 0, statins are unnecessary
- **Ignoring the TG/HDL ratio** — this simple calculation is more predictive of cardiovascular risk than LDL
