# How to Improve Male Fertility on the Animal-Based Diet

**Categories:** Fertility, Hormones, Toxin Avoidance

## TLDR

Paul Saladino frames male infertility as a consequence of metabolic dysfunction, nutrient deficiency, and environmental toxin exposure — not aging. His protocol centers on eating nose-to-tail (especially beef testicle and liver for fertility-specific nutrients), eliminating seed oils and endocrine disruptors, adding adequate carbohydrates from fruit and honey to free up bioavailable testosterone, and removing plastics, PFAS, and phthalates from the home environment.

## Background

Male fertility has declined significantly over the past several decades, mirroring the drop in testosterone levels. Saladino argues these are not separate problems — they share the same root causes: seed oils, processed food, insulin resistance, and a modern environment saturated with endocrine-disrupting chemicals. He treats male fertility as an extension of metabolic and hormonal health, not a standalone condition requiring specialized medical intervention.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Fertility-specific foods

- **Beef testicle** — contains activin, inhibin, epidermal growth factor, selenium, and zinc. Based on the "like supports like" principle: nutrients from consumed organs preferentially support the corresponding organ in the body. Activin and inhibin are involved in sex hormone formation and reproductive cell development.
- **Liver** (0.5 oz/day) — retinol (vitamin A), B vitamins, zinc, copper, choline. Retinol is essential for sperm maturation and testosterone synthesis.
- **Kidney** — selenium, which is required for sperm formation, testicular maturation, and testosterone production.
- **Heart** — CoQ10 for cellular energy and hormone synthesis.
- **Grass-fed red meat** — zinc, saturated fat (cholesterol is the backbone of all steroid hormones), stearic acid.
- **Raw dairy** (~1 liter/day) — fat-soluble vitamins A, D, K2 as hormone precursors.
- **Fruit and honey** (~300g carbs/day) — lowers SHBG to free bioavailable testosterone. On strict carnivore, Saladino's SHBG spiked to 103–120 (normal: 16–55) and testosterone dropped to ~300 ng/dL.
- **Pasture-raised egg yolks** — cholesterol, DHA, EPA.

### What harms male fertility — eliminate these

**Dietary:**
- Seed oils (phthalates, linoleic acid → insulin resistance → obesity → aromatase conversion of testosterone to estrogen)
- Processed foods and refined carbohydrates
- Strict low-carb/carnivore without fruit and honey (elevates SHBG, suppresses free testosterone)
- Alcohol and marijuana (suppress testosterone and sleep quality)

**Environmental endocrine disruptors:**
- Plastics (BPA, BPS, BPE) in food storage and water bottles
- Non-stick cookware (PFAS/Teflon) — linked to reduced sperm count
- Conventional laundry detergent — phthalates absorbed through bedding during sleep
- Polyester underwear — a study ([PMID 8279095](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8279095/)) showed significant disruption to sperm production; wear cotton or wool
- Chemical sunscreens (oxybenzone, avobenzone, octinoxate)
- Excessive heat exposure to testes (hot tubs, laptops on lap)

**Metabolic:**
- Obesity — overweight men are 1.20x more likely to be infertile; obese men 1.36x ([PMID 19692496](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19692496/)). Adipose tissue converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase.

### Supplements

- **Heart & Soil Male Fertility Stack** — Whole Package (testicle, liver, blood) + Bone Marrow & Liver + Firestarter (grass-fed suet). 6 capsules of each daily.
- **Creatine monohydrate** (5g/day) — men using creatine have significantly higher sperm counts ([PMID 35276945](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35276945/))
- **Boron** (10mg/day) — to lower elevated SHBG
- **Magnesium glycinate or threonate** (200–400mg/day) — sleep support for LH release

### Lifestyle

- Sleep 7–8 hours consistently — LH is released during sleep to signal testosterone production
- Daily sunlight — UVB activates a skin-brain-gonad axis ([PMID 34433056](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34433056/)); testes contain vitamin D receptors
- Resistance training — one of the highest-leverage testosterone interventions
- Eliminate plastics from kitchen and bathroom — use glass or stainless steel

## Key Points

- Male fertility is downstream of metabolic and hormonal health — fix insulin resistance, eliminate seed oils, and fertility improves
- Beef testicle provides tissue-specific compounds (activin, inhibin, epidermal growth factor) that directly support reproductive function
- Selenium from kidney and testicle is required for sperm formation and testicular maturation
- SHBG binds testosterone and reduces the bioavailable fraction — adequate carbohydrates from fruit and honey are essential to keep SHBG in check
- Environmental endocrine disruptors (plastics, PFAS, phthalates) cumulatively suppress fertility even when diet is optimized
- Creatine supplementation is associated with higher sperm counts and better fertility outcomes

## Common Mistakes

- **Focusing only on testosterone levels while ignoring sperm health** — fertility requires both adequate hormones and healthy sperm (count, motility, morphology)
- **Going strict carnivore for fertility** — zero-carb elevates SHBG and suppresses free testosterone, the opposite of what fertility requires
- **Ignoring environmental toxins** — diet alone won't overcome constant exposure to plastics, phthalates, and PFAS
- **Using hot tubs or saunas frequently** — excessive testicular heat impairs sperm production
- **Wearing polyester underwear** — documented disruption to sperm production; switch to cotton or wool
