# How to Improve Mood and Reduce Anxiety and Depression

**Categories:** Nutrients, Supplements, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino argues that mood disorders are rooted in neurotransmitter deficiency, gut dysfunction, and nutrient gaps — not a Prozac deficiency. He emphasizes adequate carbohydrates for serotonin production, organ meats for brain-critical nutrients like DHA and B vitamins, creatine for mental energy, sunlight for circadian regulation, and removing seed oils and plant toxins that drive neuroinflammation.

## Background

Anxiety and depression are at epidemic levels, and Saladino believes the standard American diet is a primary driver. Low-carb and carnivore diets can worsen mood because carbohydrates are required for tryptophan transport into the brain and subsequent serotonin synthesis. Simultaneously, seed oils promote neuroinflammation, gut permeability allows endotoxin to trigger systemic immune responses that affect the brain, and modern indoor lifestyles deprive people of sunlight needed for vitamin D and circadian rhythm regulation.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Eat enough carbohydrates for serotonin

Saladino frequently warns that very low-carb diets suppress serotonin production. Insulin from carbohydrate intake drives branched-chain amino acids into muscle, allowing tryptophan to cross the blood-brain barrier and convert to serotonin. He recommends 150–250g of carbs daily from fruit, honey, and raw dairy.

### Organ meats for brain nutrition

- **Beef brain** — the richest dietary source of DHA and phosphatidylserine, both critical for neuronal membrane integrity
- **Liver** — provides B12, folate, retinol, and copper, all involved in neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Heart & Soil Mood, Memory & Brain** supplement (contains brain, liver, and marrow) for those who won't eat whole organs

### Creatine for mental energy

Saladino recommends 5–10g of creatine monohydrate daily. Beyond its muscle benefits, creatine increases brain phosphocreatine stores, improving ATP availability in neurons. He cites research showing creatine supplementation reduces symptoms of depression.

### Sunlight and circadian rhythm

- Morning sunlight (within 30 minutes of waking) sets circadian rhythm and boosts cortisol for daytime alertness
- UVB exposure drives vitamin D synthesis — deficiency is strongly correlated with depression
- Sunlight triggers beta-endorphin release in the skin, producing a natural mood lift
- Avoid blue light from screens after sunset to protect melatonin production

### Remove neuroinflammatory triggers

- Seed oils generate oxidized lipid metabolites (OXLAMs) that cross the blood-brain barrier and promote neuroinflammation
- Plant defense chemicals (gluten, lectins) increase gut permeability, allowing LPS endotoxin into circulation, which activates brain microglia
- The gut-brain axis means gut health directly affects mental health — healing leaky gut often resolves mood symptoms

## Key Points

- Low-carb diets can cause or worsen depression by suppressing serotonin synthesis — adequate fruit and honey intake is essential
- DHA from beef brain or fatty fish is the primary structural fat in neuronal membranes
- Creatine is neuroprotective and has demonstrated antidepressant effects in clinical research
- Gut permeability allows bacterial endotoxin to reach the brain, activating inflammatory pathways linked to depression
- Morning sunlight is one of the most powerful free interventions for mood regulation
- Vitamin D deficiency is strongly associated with depression — sun exposure is preferred over supplementation

## Common Mistakes

- **Going very low-carb for too long** — chronic carb restriction suppresses serotonin; Saladino considers this a common cause of irritability and depression on carnivore diets
- **Ignoring gut health** — mood disorders often originate from gut permeability and dysbiosis, not just brain chemistry
- **Staying indoors** — modern indoor lifestyles eliminate the sunlight exposure needed for vitamin D, beta-endorphins, and circadian regulation
- **Relying on SSRIs without addressing root causes** — Saladino views medication as a band-aid when diet and lifestyle causes haven't been corrected
- **Avoiding all fat** — the brain is 60% fat by dry weight and requires adequate dietary fat, especially DHA and saturated fat
