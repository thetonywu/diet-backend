# What About Methylene Blue and Other Supplements to Avoid?

**Categories:** Supplements, Toxin Avoidance

## TLDR

Paul Saladino warns against several popular supplements, with methylene blue as his primary target. He argues methylene blue actually decreases ATP production in healthy mitochondria, functions as a psychiatric drug by inhibiting MAO-A, and risks serotonin syndrome when combined with common medications. He also advises against fish oil (oxidized, may increase stroke risk), multivitamins (poor bioavailability), melatonin supplements, turmeric/curcumin (high in oxalates), and nootropics generally. His position: fix the root causes through diet rather than adding supplements to mask dysfunction.

## Background

The biohacking and health optimization communities have embraced a growing list of supplements claiming to enhance mitochondrial function, cognitive performance, and longevity. Saladino challenges many of these products, arguing that they either don't work as advertised in healthy people, carry underappreciated risks, or distract from the foundational dietary and lifestyle changes that actually move the needle. His newsletter "Biohackers Might Hate Me For Saying This" directly targets the methylene blue trend.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**Methylene blue — his most detailed critique:**

Saladino's core arguments against methylene blue:
- It is synthesized from dimethylaniline, a petroleum derivative.
- Its only legitimate medical use is treating methemoglobinemia — a condition where hemoglobin cannot carry oxygen. Healthy people do not have this condition.
- In healthy mitochondria, methylene blue actually decreases ATP production. "The very supplement you're taking for more energy could be reducing your cellular energy production if your mitochondria are functioning normally."
- It strongly inhibits MAO-A (monoamine oxidase A), functioning like a psychiatric drug by increasing brain serotonin levels. This makes it impossible to know whether feeling better is from improved energy metabolism or simply altered neurotransmitter levels.
- Combining methylene blue with serotonergic medications — SSRIs, SNRIs, tricyclic antidepressants, St. John's Wort, Rhodiola, or 5-HTP — risks serotonin syndrome, which can cause heart palpitations, sweating, anxiety, and potentially death.
- At high doses (4–5 mg/kg), it can discolor brain and heart tissue.
- Studies on Alzheimer's patients showed no improved outcomes and actually increased the number of falls.
- His alternative: "Methylene blue is not the way to address metabolic dysfunction. The easy root cause solution is to cut out seed oils and processed sugars." Get CoQ10 naturally from eating heart and liver.

**Fish oil:**
- Omega-3 PUFAs are highly vulnerable to oxidation. 83% of fish oil products tested exceed recommended peroxide levels, and only 8% meet international standards.
- Some studies link regular fish oil use to increased atrial fibrillation and stroke risk (PMID 38800667).
- Saladino's own fatty acid analysis showed excellent EPA, DPA, and DHA levels eating zero fish oil — sourced entirely from grass-fed beef, tallow, pasture-raised egg yolks, raw dairy, and salmon roe.
- His alternative: eat whole fatty fish (sardines, salmon, mackerel) and grass-fed ruminant meat. Beef brain is also rich in DHA.

**Multivitamins:**
- Contain poor bioavailability forms of nutrients.
- Include industrial binders and fillers: silicon dioxide, polyvinyl pyrrolidone, hydroxypropyl methylcellulose.
- Completely unnecessary when eating organ meats. He calls liver "nature's multivitamin."

**Melatonin supplements:**
- Saladino avoids them and does not recommend them.
- His sleep protocol supports the body's own melatonin production: morning sunlight, blocking blue light after sunset, consistent sleep schedule, and finishing eating 2–3 hours before bed.
- Supplemental melatonin can suppress the body's natural production over time.

**Turmeric / curcumin:**
- Marketed as "anti-inflammatory" but turmeric is high in oxalates, a plant toxin that contributes to kidney stones and mineral depletion.
- His approach: remove the causes of inflammation (seed oils, plant toxins) rather than try to counteract them with more plant chemicals.

**Nootropics generally:**
- Warns against "using nootropics or methylene blue instead of fixing diet."
- Cognitive performance is best addressed through seed oil elimination, adequate carbohydrates, organ meats (especially brain and liver), quality sleep, and sunlight.

**Other supplement forms to avoid:**
- Magnesium oxide: poorly absorbed (as low as 4%), essentially a laxative. Use magnesium glycinate or threonate instead.
- Creatine HCl / buffered creatine: no proven advantage over standard monohydrate, more expensive for no benefit.
- Synthetic prenatal vitamins: contain less bioavailable forms of folate (folic acid vs. methylfolate from liver) and iron (ferrous sulfate vs. heme iron from meat).

## Key Points

- Methylene blue decreases ATP production in healthy mitochondria — the opposite of its marketed benefit.
- Methylene blue's MAO-A inhibition makes it function as a psychiatric drug, not a mitochondrial enhancer, in healthy people.
- Serotonin syndrome from combining methylene blue with SSRIs, SNRIs, or other serotonergic compounds is a serious and potentially fatal risk.
- 83% of fish oil products are oxidized beyond recommended limits. Oxidized omega-3 PUFAs may be actively harmful.
- The animal-based diet naturally provides the nutrients these supplements claim to deliver: CoQ10 from heart, DHA from brain and fatty fish, retinol and folate from liver.
- Saladino's supplement philosophy is food-first: "No, you absolutely do not need additional vitamins if you're eating meat and organs."
- The few supplements he does recommend — creatine monohydrate, magnesium glycinate/threonate, colostrum, and desiccated organ supplements — fill specific gaps that animal foods alone cannot fully cover.
- "Fix the root cause" is the recurring principle. Most supplement use is an attempt to patch symptoms of seed oil consumption, nutrient deficiency, and poor sleep.

## Common Mistakes

- **Taking methylene blue because a biohacker recommended it.** Without understanding the MAO-A inhibition and ATP reduction in healthy cells, users may attribute altered mood (from serotonin changes) to improved mitochondrial function.
- **Combining methylene blue with antidepressants.** This is a documented serotonin syndrome risk and can be life-threatening.
- **Taking fish oil for heart health.** The oxidation profile of most commercial products and emerging data on atrial fibrillation risk make whole fish a far safer omega-3 source.
- **Relying on multivitamins as nutritional insurance.** The bioavailability of most multivitamin ingredients is poor. A single ounce of liver provides more usable nutrition than a month of multivitamins.
- **Using turmeric supplements for inflammation.** Oxalate content creates its own problems. Address the root causes of inflammation (seed oils, gut permeability) instead.
- **Supplementing melatonin nightly.** This can suppress natural melatonin production. Fix light exposure, sleep timing, and evening meals instead.
- **Choosing the wrong form of supplements you do take.** Magnesium oxide, creatine HCl, and synthetic folic acid are all inferior to their better-absorbed alternatives.
