# How Do I Optimize Exercise and Recovery on the Animal-Based Diet?

**Categories:** Fitness, Hormones

## TLDR

Paul Saladino prioritizes resistance training and daily outdoor movement over chronic endurance exercise. His pre-workout protocol is raw milk, raw honey, creatine, and sea salt — no coffee. Recovery centers on adequate carbohydrates from fruit and honey (150–300g+ daily), quality sleep, colostrum, and magnesium. He warns that undereating carbs while training is one of the biggest mistakes, citing his own testosterone crash to ~300 ng/dL on zero-carb carnivore.

## Background

Many people come to the animal-based diet from keto or carnivore backgrounds where carbohydrates are restricted. Saladino argues this is a serious problem for anyone who exercises regularly, as chronic carb restriction elevates cortisol, suppresses testosterone and IGF-1, disrupts sleep, and ultimately causes muscle loss. His exercise and recovery framework is built around hormonal optimization through diet rather than elaborate training programs.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**Exercise types (in priority order):**
- Resistance training / heavy lifting — "one of the highest-leverage testosterone interventions." He cites research (PMID 32297287) linking resistance training to higher testosterone levels.
- Daily walking — two or three 15-minute walks around meals for blood glucose control.
- Outdoor activity — he surfs daily and emphasizes exercising outdoors for sunlight benefits. UVB exposure activates a skin-brain-gonad axis that increases testosterone (Cell Reports 2021, PMID 34433056).
- Avoid chronic endurance exercise — he explicitly warns it "may harm hormonal profiles."
- Exercise regularly but not too close to bedtime.

**Pre-workout protocol (taken before 6 AM):**
- Raw milk (goat's or cow's)
- Raw honey (glyphosate-free)
- 5g creatine monohydrate (Lineage Provisions brand)
- Sea salt (Redmond Sea Salt — enhances creatine absorption into muscle cells)
- No coffee. He considers this combination superior for sustained energy without crashes.

**Post-workout / recovery nutrition:**
- Carbohydrates are critical. Fruit and honey support glycogen replenishment and recovery.
- Preferred carb sources: watermelon, blueberries, mango, papaya, oranges, bananas, raw honey, coconut water, fresh-squeezed orange juice.
- Scale carbs to activity level (grams per lb of goal body weight):
  - Low activity (0–3 hrs/week): 0.7–0.9g/lb
  - Moderate (3–6 hrs/week): 0.8–1.1g/lb
  - High (6–12 hrs/week): 1.0–1.5g/lb
  - Very high (12+ hrs/week): 1.2–1.7g/lb
- Minimum 150g carbs/day even for sedentary people. Saladino personally eats ~300g/day.
- Protein: at least 1 lb of meat per 100 lbs body weight (~1–1.2g protein per lb of goal body weight) from grass-fed ruminant meats.

**Sleep as the recovery foundation:**
- 7–8 hours at consistent times.
- Morning sunlight to set circadian clock.
- Block blue light 1–2 hours before bed.
- Glycine from bone broth, cartilage, and connective tissue promotes deep sleep.
- Finish eating 2–3 hours before bed.

## Key Points

- Resistance training is the most important exercise modality for hormonal health and body composition.
- Chronic endurance exercise (long-distance running, excessive cardio) can harm hormonal profiles and is not recommended.
- Carbohydrates are essential for recovery — "avoiding carbs around training" is a common and costly mistake.
- Saladino's own testosterone dropped to ~300 ng/dL on strict zero-carb carnivore, then recovered to 857 ng/dL at age 48 after adding fruit and honey.
- Creatine is his #1 supplement for exercise performance — 10–20% strength improvement backed by over 1,000 peer-reviewed studies.
- Organ meats directly support athletic performance: heart provides CoQ10 for mitochondrial energy, liver provides retinol, zinc, and B12 for testosterone synthesis.
- Eat 2–3 meals in a ~10-hour window. OMAD and extreme fasting suppress testosterone and IGF-1 in active people.
- Morning electrolytes (water with 2–3g sodium, magnesium, potassium) should precede training.
- Eliminating seed oils restores mitochondrial function, which is foundational to exercise performance and recovery.

## Common Mistakes

- **Restricting carbs while training.** This chronically elevates cortisol, which is directly antagonistic to testosterone. Insufficient carbs "suppress hormones, disrupt sleep, and cause muscle loss over time."
- **Relying on coffee as a pre-workout.** Saladino replaced coffee entirely with raw milk, honey, creatine, and salt for more stable energy.
- **Doing OMAD or aggressive fasting as an active person.** One meal a day suppresses testosterone and IGF-1, undermining the hormonal environment needed for recovery and muscle growth.
- **Prioritizing cardio over resistance training.** Chronic cardio without strength training does not build the hormonal and metabolic resilience Saladino considers essential.
- **Using protein powders instead of whole food.** Grass-fed ruminant meat provides superior nutrition — protein powders are unnecessary when eating properly.
- **Neglecting sleep.** No supplement or training protocol compensates for poor sleep. Sleep is where recovery actually happens.
