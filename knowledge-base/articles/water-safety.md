# Is Your Water Safe? What to Drink on the Animal-Based Diet

**Categories:** Water, Toxin Avoidance, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino warns that most tap and bottled water contains fluoride, chlorine, microplastics, and other contaminants that disrupt hormones and gut health. He recommends mineral-rich spring water or properly filtered water, avoids plastic bottles, and emphasizes electrolyte-based hydration with sodium, potassium, and magnesium rather than drinking excessive plain water.

## Background

Water quality is rarely discussed in mainstream nutrition, but Saladino considers it foundational. Municipal tap water is treated with chlorine and often fluoridated — chemicals he views as harmful to the thyroid and microbiome. Most bottled water is stored in plastic that leaches endocrine-disrupting chemicals like BPA and phthalates. Meanwhile, the standard advice to "drink eight glasses of water a day" ignores electrolyte balance, which Saladino argues is far more important than raw water volume.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Avoid tap water without filtration

Tap water contains fluoride (a known thyroid disruptor), chlorine (damages gut bacteria), and potential contaminants like heavy metals and pharmaceutical residues. Saladino recommends never drinking unfiltered tap water.

### Filter or source properly

- **Reverse osmosis (RO)** removes fluoride, chlorine, and heavy metals — but also strips minerals, so remineralization is necessary
- **Spring water** in glass bottles is ideal — naturally mineral-rich without chemical treatment
- **Gerolsteiner** (glass bottle) is his most frequently recommended sparkling mineral water — high in calcium and magnesium
- **Topo Chico** (glass bottle) is another approved sparkling option
- Avoid plastic bottles entirely — even "BPA-free" plastics leach estrogenic compounds

### Hydration is about electrolytes, not volume

Saladino emphasizes that proper hydration depends on electrolyte balance, not simply drinking more water. Drinking excessive plain water without electrolytes can dilute sodium and worsen dehydration symptoms.

- **Sodium**: ~5–7g per day from Redmond Real Salt (unrefined, no microplastics) added to water and food
- **Potassium**: from fruit (bananas, coconut water, orange juice), bone broth, and raw dairy
- **Magnesium**: 200–400mg supplemental magnesium glycinate or threonate daily, plus mineral water
- An "adrenal cocktail" of OJ, coconut water, and Redmond salt is a staple hydration tool

### Bottled water brands to avoid

- Any water in plastic bottles (Dasani, Aquafina, etc.) — microplastics and estrogenic leaching
- Brands with added fluoride
- Alkaline water products — Saladino considers artificially alkalinized water unnecessary and potentially harmful to stomach acid

## Key Points

- Fluoride in tap water suppresses thyroid function — Saladino cites this as one reason to always filter
- Chlorine kills beneficial gut bacteria in addition to pathogens
- Plastic water bottles leach endocrine disruptors regardless of BPA-free labeling
- Drinking plain water without electrolytes can worsen hydration status by diluting sodium
- Mineral water in glass provides calcium and magnesium that RO systems strip out
- Redmond Real Salt is preferred over table salt because it's unrefined, contains trace minerals, and is sourced from an ancient sea bed (no microplastic contamination)

## Common Mistakes

- **Drinking unfiltered tap water** — fluoride and chlorine exposure accumulates over time
- **Relying on plastic-bottled water** — trading one set of contaminants for endocrine disruptors
- **Drinking excessive plain water** — without electrolytes, this can dilute sodium and cause fatigue, headaches, and cramping
- **Using table salt for electrolytes** — refined, stripped of trace minerals, and potentially contaminated with microplastics
- **Ignoring water quality while optimizing food** — Saladino considers clean water as important as clean food
