# What Supplements Do I Actually Need on the Animal-Based Diet?

**Categories:** Supplements, Animal-Based Diet Basics

## TLDR

Saladino's position is that a properly constructed animal-based diet — meat, organs, fruit, honey, and raw dairy — provides almost everything you need without synthetic supplements. The short list he does recommend: creatine monohydrate (5g/day), organ supplements if you're not eating fresh organs, colostrum for gut and immune support, and electrolytes. He actively argues against fish oil, multivitamins, and methylene blue.

## Background

Supplement culture is a minefield of overhyped products with poor evidence. Saladino is unusually skeptical for someone who sells supplements — he built his entire argument on the premise that nose-to-tail animal foods make most supplementation unnecessary. He frames organ meats as "nature's multivitamin" and argues that most nutrient deficiencies are better solved by improving your diet than adding pills. The supplements he does recommend are ones where food genuinely falls short or where the evidence is strong enough to override his food-first philosophy.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### His Philosophy

Eat organs and you won't need most supplements. He says directly: *"No, you absolutely do not need additional vitamins if you're eating meat and organs."* Synthetic multivitamins, in his view, contain poor-bioavailability forms of nutrients wrapped in industrial binders and fillers — and are completely unnecessary if your diet is right.

The exceptions are few and specific.

### Supplements He Recommends

#### Creatine Monohydrate — 5g/day
His most universally recommended supplement. The argument:
- The body produces only ~1g/day but functions optimally at higher levels
- Creatine synthesis consumes roughly 50% of the body's daily methylation capacity — supplementing frees up that capacity for neurotransmitter synthesis, immune function, and other critical processes
- Benefits: 10–20% improvement in strength and exercise performance, 5–15% cognitive gains, supports fertility and sleep quality
- Most people are chronically under-saturated

**Dose:** 5g/day. No loading phase required. He takes it every morning mixed into raw milk with honey.
**Form:** Micronized creatine monohydrate + sea salt (salt aids absorption).
**Brand:** [Lineage Provisions Creatine Monohydrate](https://lineageprovisions.com/products/creatine-monohydrate) — his own product, 99.9% pure, no fillers, artificial sweeteners, flavors, or flow agents.

#### Colostrum — 6 capsules/day
Bovine colostrum is the first milk produced in the 24–48 hours after calving. It's rich in immunoglobulins (IgA, IgG, IgM), growth factors (IGF-I and IGF-II), and lactoferrin. Saladino recommends it for gut healing, immune function, and exercise recovery. Research shows measurable benefits at doses as low as 1.5–3g/day.

**Dose:** 6 capsules/day (3,000mg). Same ramp-up protocol as organ supplements: start at 1 capsule/day, add 1 per week.

#### Electrolytes (Salt + Magnesium)
His morning protocol: a large glass of water with ~2–3g sodium, magnesium, and potassium on waking.

**Salt:** [Redmond Sea Salt](https://redmond.life/) is his primary sodium source. He estimates he consumes ~6.5g sodium/day given his high activity level.

**Magnesium:** Harder to get from animal foods alone, so supplementation is reasonable. Preferred forms are **glycinate** (well-absorbed, calming) or **threonate** (crosses the blood-brain barrier, cognitive benefits). Avoid forms that cause GI distress (magnesium oxide is the main offender). General dosing guidance: 200–400mg elemental magnesium/day. He doesn't publicly name a specific magnesium brand.

---

#### Methylated B Vitamins — Personal Use Only
Saladino personally takes methylated B vitamins (methylfolate + methylcobalamin) because he carries the **MTHFR gene mutation**, which impairs normal folate metabolism. He is explicit this is a personal edge case — he says the vast majority of people eating animal foods do not need B vitamin supplementation. B12, riboflavin, and biotin are abundant in meat and organs.

If you suspect an MTHFR mutation (common symptoms: poor stress tolerance, elevated homocysteine), testing is available and a functional medicine provider can guide you. For everyone else: eat organs.

### Supplements He Gets from Food Instead

#### Vitamin D — Sunlight First
He strongly prefers sun exposure over supplementation and argues that *"the benefits from sunlight exposure literally cannot be obtained from a vitamin D pill."* He surfs daily and gets substantial sun. For those with limited sun exposure (winter, indoor jobs): he suggests a vitamin D3 supplement in an olive oil carrier. Use the free **D-Minder app** to calculate optimal sun time for your location, skin type, and UV index.

He opposes chemical sunscreens (oxybenzone, avobenzone, octinoxate) as hormone disruptors. If you need UV protection, he recommends tallow-based sunscreens with zinc oxide.

#### Omega-3 / Fish Oil — Do Not Supplement
He has published a newsletter titled "You Will NEVER Take Fish Oil Again" and actively discourages it:
- Omega-3 PUFAs are highly vulnerable to oxidation; most commercial products are already rancid
- 83% of products tested exceed recommended peroxide levels; only 8% meet international standards
- Some studies link regular fish oil use to increased atrial fibrillation and stroke risk
- He maintains excellent EPA, DPA, and DHA levels eating no fish and no fish oil by eating grass-fed beef, tallow, pasture-raised egg yolks, and raw dairy

Get your omega-3s from food. If you eat fatty fish, wild-caught salmon and sardines are fine. He also eats salmon roe and beef brain when available.

#### K2, Zinc, Selenium, B12, Iodine
All adequately supplied by organ meats and animal foods:
- **K2** — liver, raw dairy
- **Zinc and selenium** — red meat, organs
- **B12** — all animal foods
- **Iodine** — wild-caught cod, oysters, eggs, raw dairy. Muscle meat alone is not a good iodine source, so including seafood or dairy matters

### Supplements He Says to Avoid

**Multivitamins:** Poor bioavailability forms, industrial binders and fillers (silicon dioxide, polyvinyl pyrrolidone, hydroxypropyl methylcellulose). Completely unnecessary if eating organs. Don't waste your money.

**Fish oil:** Oxidized, potentially pro-inflammatory, linked to atrial fibrillation risk. Get omega-3s from grass-fed animal foods instead.

**Methylene blue:** He is firmly against it. His arguments:
- Medically used to treat methemoglobinemia — a condition healthy people don't have
- In a healthy mitochondrial electron transport chain, methylene blue *decreases* ATP production — the opposite of the claimed benefit
- Strongly inhibits monoamine oxidase A, functioning like a psychiatric drug; disrupts serotonin metabolism and can cause serotonin syndrome (palpitations, anxiety, sweating, potentially fatal)
- His quote: *"Methylene blue is not the way to address metabolic dysfunction. The easy root cause solution is to cut out seed oils and processed sugars."*

**Melatonin:** He avoids it and doesn't recommend it. His sleep protocol relies on light management (no blue light after sunset), meal timing (finishing eating 2–3 hours before bed), and circadian rhythm alignment — not melatonin pills.

## Key Points

- An animal-based diet with regular organ meat consumption covers almost all micronutrient needs without supplements
- Creatine (5g/day via Lineage Provisions) is his one universally recommended supplement — the evidence is strong and diet doesn't cover the need
- If you're not eating fresh organs, Heart & Soil Beef Organs capsules are the place to start; ramp up slowly from 1 capsule/day
- Colostrum (Heart & Soil) is recommended for gut healing, immune function, and recovery
- Morning electrolytes (Redmond Sea Salt + magnesium) support hydration, especially on active days
- Vitamin D from sunlight, not pills; use D-Minder app to optimize sun exposure
- Fish oil is actively harmful — get omega-3s from grass-fed beef, egg yolks, raw dairy, and salmon roe
- Methylene blue is dangerous for healthy people — avoid
- Multivitamins are unnecessary and poorly absorbed — fix the diet instead

## Common Mistakes

- **Taking a multivitamin instead of eating organs** — synthetic isolated nutrients in industrial pill form are a poor substitute for the bioavailable nutrient matrix in whole animal foods
- **Taking fish oil** — most products are oxidized and potentially harmful; the rationale for supplementing is weak when grass-fed animal foods cover the need
- **Jumping to 6 Heart & Soil capsules immediately** — the liver content can cause nausea in organ-naive people; start at 1 capsule/day and increase by 1 per week
- **Not taking creatine** — it's the most evidence-backed supplement available, most people are chronically under-saturated, and it's cheap; the main reason people skip it is unfounded fear from old myths about kidney damage (not supported by evidence in healthy people)
- **Relying on sunscreen and avoiding sun** — this blocks vitamin D synthesis and the broader suite of sun benefits; build sun tolerance gradually and use zinc oxide rather than chemical sunscreens when needed
- **Taking methylene blue** — the biohacking community has strongly promoted this; Saladino's counterarguments (MAO inhibition, ATP reduction in healthy people) are worth understanding before trying it
