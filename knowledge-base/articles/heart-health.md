# Is the Animal-Based Diet Safe for Heart Health?

**Categories:** Heart Disease, Animal-Based Diet Basics

## TLDR

Saladino argues the animal-based diet is not just safe for heart health but actively cardioprotective. He believes the diet-heart hypothesis — that saturated fat and cholesterol cause heart disease — is wrong, and that the actual culprits are seed oils and insulin resistance. His own CAC score is zero despite very high LDL and a family history of early heart disease.

## Background

Cardiovascular disease is the most common objection to eating an animal-based diet. Conventional medicine links saturated fat and dietary cholesterol to heart disease, and high LDL to cardiovascular risk. Saladino challenges both of these claims directly — and backs his argument with his own cardiac imaging, extensive research citations, and input from several cardiologists and cardiac surgeons who share his skepticism.

## What Paul Saladino Says

### The Root Cause of Heart Disease

Saladino argues **insulin resistance — not LDL — is the primary driver of atherosclerosis**. His mechanistic explanation:

1. Insulin resistance causes chronic elevation of blood glucose and vascular inflammation
2. Inflamed endothelium becomes damaged and permeable
3. LDL particles enter damaged arterial walls as part of a repair process, not as a primary cause
4. In the absence of endothelial damage, LDL circulates without causing plaque

He frames it this way: LDL is "the wood" and metabolic dysfunction is "the spark." Without the spark, the wood doesn't ignite.

**What causes insulin resistance?** His answer: excess linoleic acid from seed oils. His proposed mechanism:
- Seed oils are high in linoleic acid (omega-6 PUFA), which is chemically unstable
- Excess linoleic acid accumulates in fat cells, causing them to enlarge and release free fatty acids
- Those free fatty acids induce peripheral insulin resistance in muscles
- Insulin resistance produces vascular inflammation
- Linoleic acid is also the most common oxidized fatty acid found inside LDL particles — oxidized LDL (oxLDL) is taken up by macrophages in the arterial wall, forming foam cells and plaque

He cites the **oxidized linoleic acid hypothesis** from researchers DiNicolantonio and O'Keefe (PMC6196963) as the mechanistic framework, and has collaborated extensively with Tucker Goodrich and Dr. Chris Knobbe on this argument.

### Saturated Fat Does Not Cause Heart Disease

Saladino's position is unambiguous: **saturated fat is not the cause of heart disease**. His arguments:

- Saturated fat consumption was *higher* in 1900 than today; heart disease rates were far lower — the timeline doesn't support causation
- The diet-heart hypothesis (Ancel Keys, 1950s–60s) was built on data that cherry-picked countries supporting a saturated fat correlation and excluded those that didn't
- The **Minnesota Coronary Experiment** — the most rigorous RCT testing the diet-heart hypothesis — found that replacing saturated fat with vegetable oil lowered LDL by ~14% but *increased* all-cause mortality; this data was suppressed and only published in the BMJ in 2016
- Multiple meta-analyses have failed to find a causal link between saturated fat intake and cardiovascular mortality

He argues modern dietary guidelines locked in a flawed hypothesis before adequate RCT evidence was available, and that the result has been 60 years of misplaced dietary advice.

### What Actually Protects Heart Health

**Foods and nutrients he specifically cites as cardioprotective:**

- **Vitamin K2** (menaquinone) — found in grass-fed animal fat, egg yolks, and liver. He cites the Rotterdam Study showing higher K2 (but not K1 from plants) was significantly associated with reduced coronary heart disease outcomes. K2 directs calcium into bones and away from arterial walls.
- **CoQ10** from beef heart — a mitochondrial energy compound; notably, statins block the same metabolic pathway that produces CoQ10, depleting it as a side effect
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** (EPA, DHA, DPA) — present in grass-fed beef fat; anti-inflammatory; reduces triglycerides
- **Conjugated Linoleic Acid (CLA)** — in grass-fed beef; anti-inflammatory fat
- **Eliminating seed oils** — removes the primary dietary source of linoleic acid-induced insulin resistance and LDL oxidation
- **Low insulin / metabolic health** — animal-based eating lowers fasting insulin, reduces vascular inflammation, raises HDL, and drops triglycerides

**Markers that improve on the animal-based diet (as Saladino reports):**
- Triglycerides fall to 40–70 mg/dL
- HDL rises to 70–90 mg/dL
- TG/HDL ratio drops well below 2 (his target: ~0.5)
- Fasting insulin drops to 2–5 uIU/mL
- hsCRP drops below 1 mg/L

### His Personal Cardiac Data

Saladino has made his own cardiovascular imaging results a central part of his public argument. Despite eating very high amounts of meat, saturated fat, and having LDL as high as 533 mg/dL:

- **Coronary artery calcium (CAC) score: 0** — no calcified plaque
- **CT angiography: no arterial plaque detected**
- **Family history: his father had a heart attack at age 43**

His quote: *"You are looking at my arteries — the arteries that are supposed to be full of plaque after years of eating tons of meat and saturated fat, and having an 'elevated' LDL... But there is NONE."*

He uses this as proof-of-concept: high LDL in the context of metabolic health (low fasting insulin, low hsCRP, low TG, high HDL) does not produce cardiovascular disease.

### The Markers That Actually Matter

He argues the standard lipid panel (total cholesterol, LDL-C) is the wrong tool for assessing cardiovascular risk. His preferred markers:

- **Fasting insulin** < 5 uIU/mL (his own: < 3) — the most important metabolic indicator
- **Triglycerides** ≤ 70 mg/dL
- **HDL** ≥ 60 mg/dL
- **TG/HDL ratio** < 2 (optimal: ~0.5)
- **hsCRP** < 1 mg/L — measures vascular inflammation
- **Fasting glucose** 70–85 mg/dL, HbA1c ≤ 5.3%
- **CAC scan** — he calls this the "gold standard" for actual plaque burden; a CAC of 0 in a metabolically healthy person is, in his view, sufficient to deprioritize LDL concerns

### His Position on Statins

Saladino is skeptical of statins, particularly for people who are metabolically healthy but have elevated LDL. His arguments:

- Statins increase insulin resistance and raise type 2 diabetes risk — counterproductive if insulin resistance is the actual cause of heart disease
- Statins block the mevalonate pathway, which also produces CoQ10 — contributing to muscle symptoms and cardiovascular fatigue
- Some data links statin use to cognitive decline
- A recent study found statins reduce GLP-1 levels by nearly half within 16 weeks

His prescription: **get a CAC scan before accepting a statin prescription**. If your CAC is 0 and your metabolic markers are healthy, there is no established basis for statin therapy in his view.

## Key Points

- Saladino believes heart disease is caused by insulin resistance and seed oil-driven LDL oxidation — not saturated fat or dietary cholesterol
- The animal-based diet improves the markers he considers most predictive: fasting insulin, TG/HDL ratio, and hsCRP
- Grass-fed animal foods provide specific cardioprotective nutrients: vitamin K2 (directs calcium out of arteries), CoQ10 (beef heart), and omega-3 fatty acids
- His own CAC score is 0 despite LDL as high as 533 mg/dL and a family history of early heart disease
- The diet-heart hypothesis (saturated fat → heart disease) is built on flawed data from the 1950s; the Minnesota Coronary Experiment showed replacing saturated fat with vegetable oil *increased* mortality
- Statins treat LDL — the wrong target — and introduce their own metabolic harms
- The CAC scan is the "gold standard" test; a score of 0 indicates no calcified plaque regardless of LDL level

## Common Mistakes

- **Conflating high LDL with high cardiovascular risk** — LDL level alone is not a reliable predictor of heart disease in metabolically healthy people; context matters (TG, HDL, fasting insulin, hsCRP)
- **Fearing saturated fat** — Saladino argues this is one of the most damaging nutritional myths; grass-fed animal fat contains K2, CLA, and omega-3s that support cardiovascular health
- **Not testing the right markers** — a standard LDL number without fasting insulin, hsCRP, and TG/HDL ratio gives an incomplete and potentially misleading picture
- **Accepting a statin without imaging** — he recommends getting a CAC scan first; a score of 0 changes the risk calculus entirely
- **Replacing animal fat with seed oils** — this is precisely the dietary switch he argues drove the modern heart disease epidemic; it removes K2 and CLA and adds oxidation-prone linoleic acid
- **Eating grain-fed instead of grass-fed beef** — conventional beef has a less favorable omega-6:omega-3 ratio and lower K2 content; the cardiovascular benefits Saladino cites are tied specifically to grass-fed, grass-finished animals
