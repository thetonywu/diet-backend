# How Do I Maximize Lifespan and Healthspan?

**Categories:** Blue Zones & Longevity, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino argues that longevity is primarily determined by metabolic health — not caloric restriction, plant-heavy diets, or cholesterol levels. He considers insulin resistance the root cause of chronic disease and aging, driven mainly by seed oil consumption. His longevity protocol centers on the animal-based diet, organ meats, adequate carbohydrates, daily sunlight, resistance training, quality sleep, and environmental toxin avoidance. He is skeptical of Blue Zones research and mainstream longevity interventions like extended fasting.

## Background

The longevity conversation in mainstream health is dominated by caloric restriction, plant-based diets, and pharmaceutical interventions like rapamycin. Saladino challenges much of this framework, arguing that the modern epidemic of chronic disease — heart disease, diabetes, cancer, autoimmune conditions — is not an inevitable consequence of aging but the result of a diet and lifestyle misaligned with human evolutionary biology. His approach treats healthspan and lifespan as natural byproducts of metabolic health rather than goals requiring special interventions.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**His core longevity framework:**
- Metabolic health is the single most important factor for longevity. Fasting insulin below 5 μIU/mL is the most important biomarker — far more predictive than LDL cholesterol.
- Seed oil elimination is "the most important lever to pull." Linoleic acid from seed oils damages mitochondria, drives insulin resistance, and is the primary driver of the chronic disease epidemic.
- Heart disease is caused by insulin resistance and oxidized LDL (primed by linoleic acid), not by saturated fat or total cholesterol. His own coronary artery calcium (CAC) score is 0 despite an LDL of 533 mg/dL and a family history of heart attack at age 43.

**His critique of Blue Zones:**
- Saladino is skeptical of Blue Zones data, questioning the accuracy of age records in regions like Sardinia, Okinawa, and Ikaria. Issues include pension fraud inflating centenarian counts and unreliable birth records.
- He notes that Sardinian centenarians with LDL cholesterol above 130 mg/dL had longer survival — contradicting the standard narrative that low cholesterol drives longevity.
- He challenges the plant-heavy dietary narrative associated with Blue Zones, pointing out that many centenarian populations ate substantial amounts of animal products (pork in Okinawa, sheep and goat in Sardinia).

**His views on common longevity interventions:**
- Against aggressive caloric restriction and OMAD. These suppress testosterone, IGF-1, and thyroid function — trading metabolic vitality for a theoretical lifespan extension.
- Against long-term ketosis. He views it as a hormonal stressor that raises cortisol and SHBG. His own testosterone dropped to ~300 ng/dL on strict carnivore, recovering to 857 ng/dL after adding fruit and honey.
- Moderate time-restricted eating (2–3 meals in a ~10-hour window) is fine, but he does not advocate strict intermittent fasting.
- He argues higher cholesterol in metabolically healthy people may actually correlate with longevity, citing research showing Swedish centenarians with higher cholesterol were more likely to reach 100.

**His longevity protocol:**

*Diet:*
- Grass-fed ruminant meat ~1 lb per 100 lbs body weight daily
- Organ meats: liver, heart, kidney for nutrient density
- 150–300g+ carbohydrates from fruit and honey (not keto)
- Raw dairy if tolerated
- Complete elimination of seed oils, grains, legumes, processed food

*Lifestyle:*
- Daily morning sunlight — circadian rhythm, vitamin D, nitric oxide, testosterone
- 7–8 hours sleep with circadian alignment
- Resistance training over chronic endurance exercise
- Heat exposure (sauna) for detoxification and immune function
- Cold exposure for fat metabolism and vascular health
- Nature connection: hiking, swimming, gardening

*Toxin avoidance:*
- Eliminate plastics, PFAS, phthalates from home
- Remove synthetic fragrances and conventional personal care products
- Drink spring water or properly filtered water (avoid fluoridated tap water)
- Reduce EMF exposure

*Supplements:*
- Creatine monohydrate 5g/day
- Colostrum for gut and immune health
- Magnesium glycinate/threonate 200–400mg/day
- Desiccated organ supplements (Heart & Soil) if not eating whole organs

## Key Points

- Insulin resistance — not cholesterol — is the root driver of chronic disease and premature aging. Fasting insulin below 5 μIU/mL is the target.
- Seed oils are the primary dietary contributor to metabolic dysfunction, mitochondrial damage, and the chronic disease epidemic.
- Centenarian research shows higher cholesterol levels are consistently associated with better survival in metabolically healthy populations.
- The Blue Zones narrative oversimplifies longevity and may rely on inaccurate data. Actual centenarian diets included significant animal products.
- Age-related hormonal decline (testosterone, thyroid) is "not inevitable — it's a result of poor diet, insufficient sleep, low sun exposure, and accumulated endocrine disruptors."
- Long-term caloric restriction and ketosis trade hormonal vitality for unproven lifespan extension.
- The animal-based diet positions aging as largely caused by modern inputs (seed oils, processed food, environmental toxins) rather than being an inevitable biological process.
- CAC score, fasting insulin, HbA1c, and triglyceride-to-HDL ratio are more meaningful longevity biomarkers than total LDL cholesterol.

## Common Mistakes

- **Fixating on LDL cholesterol as a longevity risk factor.** Saladino argues that among metabolically healthy people with good insulin sensitivity, the cholesterol-heart disease relationship "practically disappears."
- **Adopting extreme fasting or caloric restriction for longevity.** These interventions suppress anabolic hormones and may reduce quality of life without proven lifespan benefits in humans.
- **Following a plant-heavy diet because of Blue Zones.** The plant-forward narrative doesn't match the actual dietary patterns of many centenarian populations.
- **Staying on strict keto or carnivore long-term.** Chronic carb restriction elevates cortisol and SHBG, working against the hormonal environment needed for healthy aging.
- **Ignoring environmental toxins while optimizing diet.** Plastics, phthalates, PFAS, and synthetic chemicals accumulate and undermine metabolic health even on a perfect diet.
- **Assuming aging-related decline is normal.** Saladino maintains 857 ng/dL testosterone at age 48 without TRT, demonstrating that hormonal decline is not inevitable.
