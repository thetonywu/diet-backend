## Background

Creatine monohydrate is the single most studied sports supplement in history, with over 1,000 peer-reviewed studies. Despite this, many people skip it due to outdated myths about kidney damage or water retention. Saladino considers this a major mistake. He frames creatine not as a "gym supplement" but as a fundamental metabolic molecule that most people are chronically under-saturated in — and argues the benefits extend far beyond exercise performance into brain function, hormonal health, fertility, and sleep quality. If he could only take one supplement, this would be it.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Why "Megadose"?

The body synthesizes only ~1g of creatine per day from arginine, glycine, and methionine. Saladino takes 5g/day — roughly 5x what the body makes — which is why people describe it as a megadose. In clinical terms, 5g/day is actually the standard research dose, but relative to endogenous production it's a significant increase. An optional loading phase of 20g/day (5g x 4 servings) for 5-7 days can saturate muscles faster, followed by 3-5g/day maintenance.

### The Methylation Argument

This is Saladino's core argument for why creatine is more than a gym supplement:

- Creatine synthesis from arginine, glycine, and methionine consumes nearly **50% of the body's entire daily methylation capacity**
- Methylation (via SAMe — S-adenosylmethionine) is required for neurotransmitter production, DNA repair, immune function, detoxification, and hormone metabolism
- By supplementing creatine externally, you eliminate the need for endogenous synthesis, freeing up that 50% of methylation capacity for everything else
- Saladino reports personal benefits: *"I've even been sleeping better and have less of that mental chatter before bed (likely thanks to the freed up methylation capacity)"*

### His Daily Protocol

- **Dose:** 5g/day, every day (training and rest days)
- **Timing:** Every morning before 6:00 AM, pre-activity
- **Mixed with:** Raw goat's or cow's milk + glyphosate-free honey
- **Form:** Micronized creatine monohydrate + sea salt (salt enhances absorption into muscle cells)
- **Brand:** [Lineage Provisions Creatine Monohydrate](https://lineageprovisions.com/products/creatine-monohydrate) — 99.9% pure, third-party tested for heavy metals, glyphosate, and pesticides. No artificial sweeteners, flavors, colors, flow agents, or anti-caking agents.
- **Timeline:** Effects noticeable within the first week; significant improvements after 2-4 weeks

### The Evidence He Cites

- **Strength and power:** 10-20% improvements in high-intensity exercise performance
- **Cognition:** 5-15% improvements in memory tasks, enhanced mental clarity, and better recall. A single large dose of creatine can improve brain function even when sleep-deprived.
- **Fertility:** Men using creatine have significantly higher sperm counts and better fertility outcomes (he cites PMID: 35276945)
- **Sleep:** Better sleep quality, potentially through freed methylation capacity
- **Recovery:** Reduced inflammation and faster muscle recovery
- **Heart health:** Potential cardiovascular support
- **Mood:** Depression symptom reduction

### Kidney Safety

Saladino dismisses kidney damage concerns as an unfounded myth not supported by evidence in healthy people. The old concern stems from the fact that creatine is metabolized to creatinine, which is a standard kidney function marker — but elevated creatinine from creatine supplementation reflects increased intake, not kidney damage. No evidence of harm exists in healthy populations at standard doses.

## Common Mistakes

- **Only taking creatine on training days** — the cognitive, hormonal, and methylation benefits require daily saturation; take it every day
- **Avoiding creatine due to kidney myths** — no evidence of harm in healthy people; the creatinine elevation is from intake, not organ damage
- **Buying creatine with fillers and artificial ingredients** — many commercial products contain artificial sweeteners, flavors, flow agents, and anti-caking agents; look for pure micronized creatine monohydrate
- **Skipping it because you think it's "just for bodybuilders"** — Saladino's argument centers on methylation, cognition, and fertility, not just muscle performance
- **Not giving it enough time** — meaningful improvements in strength and cognition build over 2-4 weeks of consistent daily use
- **Using forms other than monohydrate** — creatine HCl, buffered creatine, and other forms are more expensive with no proven advantage over standard monohydrate
