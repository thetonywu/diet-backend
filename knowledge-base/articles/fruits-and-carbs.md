# What Fruits and Carbs Are Allowed on the Animal-Based Diet?

**Categories:** Animal-Based Diet Basics, Carbohydrates & Fructose

## TLDR

Fruit, honey, coconut water, and squash are Paul Saladino's preferred carbohydrate sources on the animal-based diet. He actively encourages eating plenty of carbs — at least 150g/day, and up to 300g+ for active people — and considers whole-food fructose from fruit and honey to be healthy, not harmful. Nightshades, grains, and refined sugars are excluded.

## Background

One of the most confusing aspects of the animal-based diet for newcomers is where carbohydrates fit. Many people assume it's a low-carb or keto-adjacent diet, but Saladino has moved away from that framing and now actively advocates for significant carbohydrate intake from specific sources. He also controversially defends fructose and fruit juice, challenging mainstream low-carb and anti-sugar narratives.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### The Guiding Principle

Saladino's framework is that plants produce defense chemicals (toxins, anti-nutrients) in their leaves, seeds, stems, and roots. But fruits are what the plant *wants* animals to eat — to spread seeds — so they have the least toxins. He calls fruit "the best way to get carbohydrates with the least amount of plant toxins."

### Recommended Carb Sources

**Top fruits (eat freely):**
- **Watermelon** — he makes fresh watermelon juice daily; calls it a functional food rich in lycopene
- **Blueberries** — 550g/day in his personal plan
- **Mango** — personal favorite, eaten sliced regularly
- **Papaya** — 250g/day in his personal plan; rich in digestive enzymes
- **Orange / fresh-squeezed OJ** — he drinks it daily; cites research showing it raises antioxidant status and reduces lipid peroxidation
- **Banana, pineapple, pear, apple, grapes, dates, berries** — all recommended

**Other approved carb sources:**
- **Honey** (raw, unfiltered, local) — ~10 tablespoons/day in his personal meal plan; he calls it "a health food" and puts it on steak or in raw milk daily. His preferred brand is **Lineage Provisions** raw organic honey; he recommends the darkest, most local raw honey you can find.
- **Coconut water** — daily carb source
- **Squash** (flesh only, no skin or seeds) — 200g/day in his personal plan
- **Fresh-squeezed fruit juices** — he has a dedicated podcast episode defending fruit juice, citing cardiovascular benefits from grape, pomegranate, cherry, and orange juice

### How Much to Eat

Saladino provides an activity-based formula. His key message: **"The biggest mistake people make on animal-based diets with carbohydrates is not getting enough of them."**

| Activity Level | Hours/week | Carbs per lb of body weight |
|---|---|---|
| Low | 0–3 hrs | 0.7–0.9g/lb |
| Medium | 3–6 hrs | 0.8–1.1g/lb |
| High | 6–12 hrs | 1.0–1.5g/lb |
| Very High | 12+ hrs | 1.2–1.7g/lb |

His floor recommendation is **150g carbs/day** for sedentary people. He personally eats ~300g/day as an active surfer.

### What to Avoid

- **Nightshade fruits** (tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, goji berries) — contain lectins and solanine; associated with autoimmune issues
- **White potatoes** — contain solanine and chaconine (glycoalkaloids); he experienced brain fog when he experimented with them
- **White rice** — he personally avoids it due to arsenic accumulation; acknowledges some people tolerate it
- **Beets, garlic, onions, turmeric** — oxalates and sulfur compounds
- **High-fructose corn syrup and refined sugars** — "avoid at all costs"; not equivalent to fructose in whole foods
- **Grains** — excluded entirely

### His Stance on Fructose

Saladino originally followed a strict zero-carb carnivore diet and was broadly anti-fructose. After about five years, he developed heart palpitations, low testosterone, joint pain, and sleep issues, which resolved when he reintroduced fruit and honey. He now argues:

- Less than 1% of dietary fructose is converted to fat in the liver — "the idea that fructose is a fat-making molecule is misleading"
- Fructose in whole-food form (fruit, honey) is not metabolically equivalent to HFCS
- Eating reasonable amounts of fruit does not cause weight gain, elevated blood pressure, or uric acid problems
- If you're eating adequate animal foods (providing choline, B vitamins), your body handles fructose smoothly

He directly challenged Robert Lustig's anti-fructose framing in podcast episode 245, arguing that the research used to condemn fructose was based on isolated fructose in industrial doses, not whole-food consumption.

## Key Points

- Fruit is the preferred carb source — it has fewer plant toxins than other plant foods because the plant "wants" it eaten
- Aim for at least 150g carbs/day; active people should target 250–300g+
- Watermelon, blueberries, mango, papaya, and fresh-squeezed OJ are Saladino's personal daily staples
- Raw, unfiltered, local honey (~10 tablespoons/day) is a major calorie and carb source; **Lineage Provisions** is his brand
- Fresh-squeezed juice is fine — he defends watermelon, orange, grape, and pomegranate juice specifically
- Squash flesh (no skin/seeds) counts as an approved carb; sweet potato is tolerated by some but not his personal choice
- White potatoes are excluded due to glycoalkaloids; white rice is personally avoided due to arsenic but considered "the least bad grain"
- Nightshades (tomatoes, peppers) are excluded despite being botanical fruits
- Whole-food fructose is benign; HFCS and refined sugars are harmful — don't conflate them

## Common Mistakes

- **Going too low-carb** — many newcomers undereat carbs; Saladino explicitly warns this suppresses hormones, disrupts sleep, and causes muscle loss over time
- **Avoiding fruit because of sugar fear** — the fructose in whole fruit is not equivalent to HFCS; research does not support avoiding fruit
- **Eating nightshades thinking they count as fruit** — tomatoes, peppers, and eggplant are botanical fruits but are excluded due to lectins and solanine
- **Choosing sweet potato over squash** — squash flesh is preferred; sweet potato is "okay for some people" but not Saladino's recommendation
- **Using pasteurized or filtered honey** — processing destroys beneficial enzymes, antioxidants, and minerals; always use raw, unfiltered honey
- **Avoiding fruit juice** — Saladino is an explicit defender of fresh-squeezed juice; the concern about juice "lacking fiber" is not a meaningful objection in his framework
- **Eating dried fruit with additives** — dried fruit is acceptable, but check for added sugars, oils, or preservatives
