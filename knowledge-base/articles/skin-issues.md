# How to Clear Up Skin Issues (Acne, Eczema, Psoriasis) on the Animal-Based Diet

**Categories:** Gut Health, Chronic Disease

## TLDR

Paul Saladino argues that skin problems are symptoms of internal dysfunction — primarily seed oil consumption, plant toxin exposure, and gut permeability — not a dermatological issue to treat topically. His protocol is to eliminate seed oils and most plant foods, eat nutrient-dense animal foods (especially liver for vitamin A), heal the gut, and let the skin clear from the inside out. Most people see significant improvement within 30–90 days.

## Background

Acne, eczema, and psoriasis are among the most common reasons people seek out the animal-based diet. Conventional dermatology typically treats these conditions topically (creams, retinoids) or systemically (antibiotics, Accutane, immunosuppressants), but many people find these treatments only manage symptoms without addressing root causes. Saladino's approach treats skin conditions as downstream effects of dietary and gut dysfunction, making them a compelling entry point for the animal-based diet.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

Saladino's skin protocol is essentially the full animal-based diet with particular emphasis on specific mechanisms:

**The root causes he identifies:**
- **Seed oils (linoleic acid)** are the primary driver. Excess linoleic acid gets incorporated into sebum, making it prone to oxidation. Oxidized sebum creates an inflammatory environment that feeds C. acnes bacteria and triggers acne. He considers this the single most impactful factor.
- **Plant toxins** — lectins, oxalates, saponins, and other defense chemicals damage the gut lining, causing intestinal permeability ("leaky gut"). Bacterial endotoxins and food proteins then enter the bloodstream, triggering immune responses that manifest as eczema, psoriasis, and inflammatory acne.
- **Conventional dairy** — pasteurized, A1-casein dairy can be inflammatory for some people and worsen skin conditions. Raw, A2 dairy is generally tolerated.

**Dietary protocol for clearing skin:**
- Eliminate all seed oils, grains, legumes, nuts, seeds, and most vegetables completely.
- Eat grass-fed ruminant meat (~1 lb per 100 lbs body weight daily).
- Eat liver (0.5 oz/day) for bioavailable retinol (vitamin A) — the most important nutrient for skin cell turnover and repair.
- Include bone marrow for collagen, fat-soluble vitamins, and stem cell factors.
- Eat ripe, low-toxin fruit (berries, watermelon, papaya, citrus) for vitamin C and antioxidants.
- Use raw dairy if tolerated; eliminate all dairy if skin doesn't improve within 30 days.
- Cook exclusively with tallow, butter, or ghee.

## Key Points

- Skin conditions are a reflection of internal inflammation and gut dysfunction, not a surface-level problem.
- Seed oil elimination is the single highest-impact change for skin health. Linoleic acid in sebum oxidizes and drives inflammatory acne.
- Vitamin A (retinol) from liver is the most important nutrient for skin — it regulates cell turnover, moisture, and repair. Plant-based beta-carotene converts poorly to retinol.
- The gut-skin axis is central: leaky gut allows endotoxins into the bloodstream, triggering immune responses that appear as eczema, psoriasis, and acne.
- Nightshades (tomatoes, peppers, eggplant) are particularly problematic for autoimmune skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis.
- Most people see significant skin clearing within 30–90 days of full dietary change. More chronic conditions like severe psoriasis may take 3–6 months.
- Linoleic acid has a long half-life in adipose tissue (~1–2 years), so full tissue replacement takes time, but functional improvements come much sooner.

## Common Mistakes

- **Not eliminating seed oils completely.** Eating out at restaurants where food is cooked in seed oils undermines the entire protocol. This is the #1 mistake.
- **Treating skin topically instead of internally.** Creams, antibiotics, and Accutane address symptoms, not root causes.
- **Continuing to eat "healthy" plant foods.** Salads, nuts, and seeds contain the plant toxins driving gut permeability.
- **Skipping organ meats.** Muscle meat alone lacks the vitamin A, collagen, and glycosaminoglycans critical for skin repair.
- **Not giving it enough time.** The body needs weeks to months to clear stored linoleic acid from sebum and heal gut lining.
- **Using conventional dairy instead of raw.** Pasteurized A1 dairy can be inflammatory and worsen acne for sensitive individuals.
- **Not eating enough animal fat.** Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K2) require dietary fat for absorption and are essential for skin health.
