# Which Fish Should I Eat and Which Should I Avoid?

**Categories:** Animal-Based Diet Basics, Toxin Avoidance

## TLDR

Paul Saladino is notably skeptical of fish compared to most nutrition voices. He places fish in a "medium toxicity" tier alongside chicken and pork — tolerable in moderation (no more than twice a week, wild-caught only) but not necessary. He argues that everything fish provides, including omega-3s, can be obtained from grass-fed ruminant meat and organs without the mercury and microplastic risks. Fish oil supplements are actively discouraged.

## Background

Fish is widely regarded as one of the healthiest protein sources, primarily for its omega-3 fatty acid content. Saladino challenges this consensus, arguing that large predatory fish bioaccumulate mercury and other heavy metals through biomagnification, that farmed fish are contaminated with antibiotics and microplastics, and that the omega-3 benefits of fish can be fully replicated through grass-fed beef, organs, and animal fats — without the toxicity risks.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Fish to eat (if you choose to eat fish)

| Fish | Why |
|---|---|
| Sardines | Very low on food chain, minimal mercury — his #1 pick |
| Anchovies | Small fish, low heavy metal accumulation |
| Wild-caught salmon | Lower mercury than large predators; limit to 2x/week |
| Wild-caught cod | Good iodine source |
| Salmon roe | Whole-food omega-3 source he personally eats when available |
| Oysters | Iodine and zinc source; acceptable in moderation |

### Fish and seafood to avoid

- **Tuna** — large predatory fish that bioaccumulates mercury through biomagnification
- **Swordfish** — same concern; very high mercury levels
- **All large predatory fish** — mercury and heavy metals concentrate up the food chain
- **Farmed fish of any kind** — contaminated with heavy metals, microplastics, and antibiotics
- **Shellfish and bottom feeders** — elevated cadmium and lead levels

### His overall position

Saladino does not actively recommend fish. He personally does not eat fish regularly and gets all his omega-3s from grass-fed beef, tallow, egg yolks, and raw dairy. His reasoning: when you eliminate seed oils, the body can efficiently convert ALA from animal fat into EPA, DPA, and DHA because omega-6 and omega-3 share conversion pathways — removing omega-6 frees the pathway. His own fatty acid analysis showed excellent DPA, DHA, and EPA levels eating zero fish and zero fish oil.

If someone chooses to eat fish, the rules are: wild-caught only, small species low on the food chain, no more than twice a week, and get heavy metal levels tested if eating fish regularly — especially if pregnant or trying to conceive.

### Fish oil supplements

Fish oil is one of the supplements he most strongly opposes:

- 83% of fish oil products tested exceeded recommended peroxide levels
- Only 8% met international oxidation standards
- Omega-3 PUFAs are highly susceptible to oxidation due to their double bonds
- Regular fish oil use is linked to increased atrial fibrillation and stroke risk ([PMID 38800667](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38800667/))
- His alternative: eat grass-fed ruminant meat and organs instead

## Key Points

- Fish is placed in the "medium toxicity" tier on the Heart & Soil food list, alongside conventional pork and chicken
- Mercury biomagnification means large predatory fish (tuna, swordfish) are the most contaminated — the higher on the food chain, the greater the risk
- Farmed fish adds antibiotics, microplastics, and heavy metal contamination on top of the baseline risks
- Omega-3s from fish are not necessary when eating grass-fed ruminant meat and eliminating seed oils
- Fish oil supplements are oxidized before they reach consumers, making them potentially pro-inflammatory — the opposite of their intended purpose
- Oysters are an exception worth noting: they provide iodine and zinc with lower contamination risk than fin fish

## Common Mistakes

- **Eating fish daily for omega-3s** — unnecessary risk of mercury accumulation; grass-fed meat provides what you need
- **Taking fish oil supplements** — most are oxidized and potentially harmful; linked to atrial fibrillation risk
- **Choosing farmed salmon over no fish at all** — farmed fish has significant contamination issues; if you can't get wild-caught, skip it
- **Assuming all fish are equivalent** — a sardine and a tuna have vastly different mercury profiles; size and position on the food chain matter
- **Not testing heavy metals** — if you eat fish regularly, Saladino recommends getting mercury levels checked, especially before or during pregnancy
