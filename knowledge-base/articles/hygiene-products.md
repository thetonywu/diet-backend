# What Hygiene Products Are Safe to Use?

**Categories:** Hygiene & Personal Care, Toxin Avoidance

## TLDR

Paul Saladino advocates an extreme minimalist approach to personal care: water only for hair and body, a natural boar's hair toothbrush with no toothpaste, beef tallow as moisturizer, and mineral (zinc oxide) sunscreen only when needed. He argues that conventional hygiene products are a major source of endocrine-disrupting chemicals — parabens, phthalates, triclosan, and synthetic fragrances — that accumulate in the body and undermine hormonal health even when diet is optimized.

## Background

The average woman uses 12 personal care products daily, exposing herself to over 160 unique chemicals. Saladino argues that skin is highly absorbent and that many of these chemicals — particularly those in shampoo, deodorant, lotion, and cosmetics — enter the bloodstream and disrupt the endocrine system. His approach treats personal care as part of the broader toxin avoidance framework: cleaning up what goes on your body is just as important as cleaning up what goes in it.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

Saladino's personal care philosophy is radically minimalist. He eliminates most conventional products entirely rather than switching to "cleaner" versions.

**By product category:**

- **Hair care:** No shampoo, no conditioner. Water only. He argues that stripping natural oils with detergent-based shampoos creates a cycle of overproduction and dependency. Hair health comes from nutrition (liver, bone marrow, collagen), not topical products.
- **Body wash / soap:** Water only for daily washing. No conventional soap or body wash.
- **Moisturizer:** Beef tallow applied directly to skin. Tallow's fatty acid profile closely matches human skin sebum, making it a superior moisturizer to petroleum-based lotions.
- **Toothpaste:** None. He recommends a natural boar's hair toothbrush with water only. Conventional toothpaste contains fluoride, which he considers a thyroid disruptor. He also advises against mouthwash, which kills beneficial oral bacteria responsible for nitric oxide production and blood pressure regulation.
- **Sunscreen:** Avoid chemical sunscreens containing oxybenzone, avobenzone, and octinoxate — all are hormone disruptors absorbed into the bloodstream. Use tallow-based mineral sunscreen with zinc oxide only when extended UV exposure is unavoidable. Eliminating seed oils from the diet reduces sunburn susceptibility by lowering linoleic acid in skin tissue.
- **Deodorant:** Avoid conventional deodorants and antiperspirants containing aluminum and synthetic fragrances. Natural alternatives or none at all — he argues body odor improves significantly on the animal-based diet as metabolic health improves.
- **Laundry detergent:** Avoid conventional detergent containing phthalates, which are absorbed through skin via clothing and bedding during sleep. Use white vinegar as a laundry alternative.
- **Cleaning products:** Replace chemical cleaners with baking soda and vinegar.
- **Clothing:** 100% cotton or wool only. Avoid polyester underwear — research (PMID 8279095) shows polyester next to testes disrupted sperm production.

**Ingredients to avoid across all products:**
- Parabens
- Phthalates
- Triclosan
- Synthetic fragrances (companies hide dozens of toxic chemicals under the single word "fragrance")
- PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances)
- Oxybenzone and other chemical UV filters
- Fluoride
- Aluminum

## Key Points

- Saladino's approach is elimination, not substitution. He uses almost no personal care products rather than seeking "clean" alternatives.
- Conventional shampoos, soaps, and lotions are absorbed through the skin and deliver endocrine-disrupting chemicals directly into the bloodstream.
- Fluoride in toothpaste disrupts thyroid function and may calcify the pineal gland. He brushes with water only using a boar's hair brush.
- Mouthwash kills beneficial oral bacteria that produce nitric oxide — a critical molecule for cardiovascular health and blood pressure regulation.
- Beef tallow is his recommended moisturizer because its fatty acid profile closely matches human skin sebum.
- Chemical sunscreens (oxybenzone, avobenzone, octinoxate) are absorbed systemically and act as hormone disruptors. Zinc oxide mineral sunscreen is the only acceptable option when sun protection is needed.
- Eliminating seed oils from the diet reduces sunburn susceptibility, reducing the need for sunscreen.
- Skin, hair, and nail health are primarily driven by nutrition — especially liver (retinol), bone marrow, and collagen — not by topical products.
- New York State banned some popular laundry detergent brands for containing 1,4-dioxane, a probable carcinogen.
- "Household exposures undermine health even on a perfect diet" — personal care product cleanup is a necessary complement to dietary changes.

## Common Mistakes

- **Switching to "natural" or "clean" branded products without reading labels.** Many "natural" products still contain synthetic fragrances, parabens, or other problematic ingredients. The label "fragrance" alone can hide dozens of chemicals.
- **Thinking you need shampoo and soap.** Water alone is sufficient for most people once the scalp and skin adjust (typically 2–4 weeks). Hair health comes from diet, not products.
- **Using chemical sunscreen daily.** Routine chemical sunscreen use exposes you to endocrine disruptors and blocks beneficial UVB rays needed for vitamin D synthesis. Reserve mineral sunscreen for extended exposure only.
- **Ignoring laundry detergent and clothing materials.** These are constant skin contact points — phthalates from detergent and chemicals from synthetic fabrics absorb through the skin during sleep.
- **Expecting topical products to fix skin and hair issues.** Saladino's framework treats these as internal problems. Acne, eczema, and hair loss improve from dietary changes (eliminating seed oils, eating liver, healing the gut), not from better creams and serums.
- **Using mouthwash for oral health.** It destroys the oral microbiome and impairs nitric oxide production, potentially raising blood pressure.
