# How Do I Reverse or Prevent Chronic Disease?

**Categories:** Chronic Disease, Seed Oils, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino argues that virtually all chronic disease — type 2 diabetes, heart disease, autoimmune conditions, obesity, NAFLD, and metabolic syndrome — shares a single root cause: insulin resistance driven primarily by seed oil consumption. His reversal protocol is the animal-based diet: eliminate seed oils and processed food, eat grass-fed ruminant meat and organs, include fruit and honey for carbohydrates, heal the gut, and remove environmental toxins. He positions chronic disease as a modern phenomenon caused by dietary and lifestyle misalignment with human evolutionary biology, not an inevitable consequence of aging.

## Background

Chronic disease now accounts for the majority of deaths and healthcare spending in the developed world. Saladino challenges the mainstream approach of managing these conditions with pharmaceuticals, arguing that they are fundamentally caused by the modern diet and environment. His framework unifies conditions that are typically treated as separate diseases — diabetes, heart disease, autoimmune disorders, obesity, fatty liver — under a single metabolic dysfunction model driven by seed oils, processed food, plant toxins, and environmental chemicals.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**The unified root cause model:**
- Seed oils (linoleic acid) are "the biggest driver of chronic disease." Linoleic acid accumulates in fat cells, generates OXLAMs (oxidized linoleic acid metabolites) that damage mitochondria, and drives insulin resistance.
- Insulin resistance is the hallmark of metabolic syndrome and underlies type 2 diabetes, obesity, cardiovascular disease, NAFLD, and hormonal dysfunction. Fasting insulin > 5 μIU/mL is a red flag.
- Plant defense chemicals (lectins, oxalates, gluten) damage the gut lining, causing permeability that allows LPS endotoxin into circulation. This triggers systemic inflammation and autoimmune conditions (eczema, psoriasis, joint pain, Hashimoto's).
- Environmental toxins (plastics, PFAS, phthalates, fluoride) accumulate and compound the metabolic damage from diet.

**The reversal protocol:**

*Phase 1 — Elimination (first 30 days):*
- Remove all seed oils, processed food, grains, legumes, and most vegetables.
- A strict carnivore phase may be useful as a strategic elimination protocol for severe autoimmune conditions or gut issues. This provides the cleanest baseline by removing all plant compounds.
- Cook only with tallow, butter, or ghee.

*Phase 2 — Animal-based diet (ongoing):*
- Grass-fed ruminant meat: at least 1 lb per 100 lbs body weight daily.
- Organ meats: liver (0.5–1 oz/day), heart (CoQ10), kidney (selenium for thyroid).
- Bone broth: 1 cup daily for glycine, collagen, and gut healing.
- Low-toxicity fruit and honey: 150–300g carbs/day. Honey may actually improve insulin sensitivity.
- Raw dairy if tolerated.
- Pasture-raised eggs.

*Phase 3 — Environmental cleanup:*
- Replace non-stick cookware with cast iron or stainless steel.
- Replace plastic food storage with glass.
- Filter water (reverse osmosis) or drink spring water.
- Switch to non-toxic personal care and cleaning products.

**Key biomarkers to track:**
- Fasting insulin (target < 5 μIU/mL, ideal < 3)
- HbA1c
- Triglycerides (target ≤ 70 mg/dL)
- HDL (target ≥ 60 mg/dL)
- TG/HDL ratio (target < 1.5)
- hsCRP (target < 1 mg/L)
- Liver enzymes / GGT
- Uric acid

**Supplements for disease reversal:**
- Creatine monohydrate 5g/day — frees methylation capacity for DNA repair and immune function.
- Colostrum — repairs gut tight junctions and modulates immune response.
- Magnesium glycinate or threonate 200–400mg/day.
- Heart & Soil organ supplements if not eating fresh organs.

## Key Points

- Chronic disease is not a collection of separate conditions — it is interconnected metabolic dysfunction driven by shared root causes.
- Seed oil elimination is the single most impactful dietary change for reversing metabolic dysfunction. Linoleic acid takes 2–4 years to fully clear from body fat.
- Fasting insulin is the most sensitive early marker of metabolic dysfunction — standard lab ranges allow up to 20 μIU/mL, which Saladino considers "dangerously loose."
- Autoimmune conditions (eczema, psoriasis, joint pain, inflammatory bowel disease) are driven by gut permeability from plant toxins and seed oils. They often resolve within 30–90 days when triggers are removed.
- The standard approach of managing chronic disease with pharmaceuticals (statins, metformin, biologics) addresses symptoms without correcting the underlying metabolic dysfunction.
- Honey may improve insulin sensitivity — contradicting the assumption that all sugar worsens metabolic disease. The issue is refined fructose (HFCS) and seed oils, not whole-food carbohydrates.
- Saladino's personal biomarkers demonstrate the protocol: CAC score 0, fasting insulin ~3, testosterone 857 ng/dL at age 48.
- Long-term ketosis is not recommended. While a short carnivore elimination phase can be strategic, chronic carb restriction elevates cortisol and suppresses thyroid and reproductive hormones.

## Common Mistakes

- **Treating symptoms with medication without addressing diet.** Statins, metformin, and biologics manage numbers without fixing the metabolic dysfunction that caused them.
- **Assuming chronic disease is genetic and inevitable.** Saladino argues that genetic predisposition is activated by dietary and environmental triggers. Remove the triggers and the expression often resolves.
- **Replacing seed oils with "healthy" plant oils.** While olive oil is less harmful than seed oils, animal fats (tallow, butter, ghee) are the recommended primary cooking fats.
- **Staying on strict keto or carnivore indefinitely.** The elimination phase is a tool, not a permanent diet. Chronic carb restriction creates its own hormonal dysfunction.
- **Ignoring environmental toxins while fixing diet.** Plastics, PFAS, and phthalates compound metabolic damage even on a perfect diet.
- **Expecting overnight reversal.** Linoleic acid takes years to clear from fat tissue. Gut healing takes 30–90 days for mild cases, longer for severe autoimmune conditions. Patience and consistency are required.
- **Using fasting glucose instead of fasting insulin to assess metabolic health.** Fasting glucose can remain normal until insulin resistance is severe. Fasting insulin catches dysfunction years earlier.
