# How Do I Improve Hair Growth and Stop Hair Loss?

**Categories:** Nutrients, Hormones

## TLDR

Saladino views hair loss as a symptom of internal dysfunction — primarily nutrient deficiencies, gut permeability, thyroid problems, and chronic inflammation driven by seed oils and plant toxins. His approach is inside-out: fix the root causes with nose-to-tail animal nutrition rather than treating hair topically. Liver, bone marrow, and the Heart & Soil Skin, Hair & Nails supplement are his primary recommendations.

## Background

Hair loss affects roughly 50% of men and 25% of women by age 50. Conventional treatments focus on blocking DHT (finasteride) or stimulating follicles topically (minoxidil), but Saladino's framework treats hair loss the same way he treats skin issues — as an external manifestation of internal problems. In his view, hair follicles starved of nutrients, bathed in inflammatory metabolites from seed oils, and impaired by thyroid dysfunction cannot grow properly regardless of what you apply topically.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

Saladino treats hair, skin, and nails as interconnected — they share the same nutritional requirements and respond to the same root causes. His protocol mirrors his skin-clearing approach.

**Step 1: Remove the triggers**
- Eliminate all seed oils — linoleic acid damages mitochondria and cell membranes, driving systemic inflammation that manifests in hair follicle dysfunction
- Remove plant toxins — oxalates (spinach, kale), lectins (nightshades), and goitrogens (cruciferous vegetables) that damage gut lining and impair thyroid function
- Avoid endocrine disruptors — plastics (BPA), non-stick cookware (PFAS), chemical sunscreens, synthetic fragrances, and conventional personal care products that disrupt hormonal balance

**Step 2: Provide the building blocks**
- **Liver** (0.5 oz/day or 2–3 oz/week) — provides retinol (vitamin A), biotin, zinc, folate, iron, and B12, all critical for hair follicle cycling
- **Bone marrow** — rich in collagen, fat-soluble vitamins, and stem cell factors that support connective tissue regeneration
- **Bone broth** (1 cup/day) — glycine and collagen for gut repair and connective tissue support
- **Grass-fed ruminant meat** (~1 lb per 100 lbs body weight) — heme iron, zinc, B vitamins, and complete protein
- **Heart and kidney** — CoQ10 for cellular energy, selenium for thyroid function
- **Pasture-raised eggs** — choline, biotin (cook whites to neutralize avidin, which blocks biotin absorption), and fat-soluble vitamins
- **Raw dairy** — fat-soluble vitamins A, D, and K2
- **Fruit** (berries, citrus, papaya) — vitamin C for collagen synthesis

**Step 4: Address the thyroid connection**
- Thyroid dysfunction is a major cause of hair loss, and Saladino identifies several dietary causes: cruciferous vegetable goitrogens, fluoridated water, and very low-carb diets that suppress thyroid function
- Adequate carbohydrates from fruit and honey (150–250g/day for active individuals) support thyroid hormone conversion
- Selenium from kidney supports thyroid function

## Key Points

- Hair loss is an internal problem — treating it topically without fixing nutrition, gut health, and hormones addresses symptoms, not causes
- Liver is the single most important food for hair — it provides retinol, biotin, zinc, iron, folate, and B12 in bioavailable forms
- Seed oils drive the systemic inflammation that damages hair follicles; eliminating them is step one
- Collagen from bone marrow, bone broth, and cartilage provides the structural proteins hair needs
- Thyroid dysfunction is a common, overlooked cause of hair loss — goitrogens from cruciferous vegetables, fluoridated water, and very low-carb diets can all impair thyroid function
- Cook egg whites thoroughly to avoid avidin blocking biotin absorption — biotin deficiency causes hair loss
- Gut permeability (leaky gut) allows endotoxin into the bloodstream, triggering immune responses that can manifest as hair loss
- Heart & Soil Skin, Hair & Nails is the dedicated supplement — full-spectrum collagen Types I–V plus liver and bone marrow nutrients
- Most people see significant skin improvement within 30–90 days of full dietary change; hair growth takes longer due to the hair growth cycle (3–6 months minimum)

## Common Mistakes

- **Treating hair loss only topically** — creams, serums, and topical treatments address symptoms while ignoring the nutritional and inflammatory root causes
- **Not eating liver** — liver provides nearly every nutrient critical for hair growth; no supplement fully replaces it
- **Eating raw egg whites** — avidin in raw whites binds biotin and prevents absorption; always cook the whites (yolks can be runny)
- **Staying very low-carb** — strict carnivore or keto without adequate carbs can suppress thyroid function, which directly causes hair loss
- **Eating cruciferous vegetables for "health"** — broccoli, cauliflower, kale, and Brussels sprouts contain goitrogens that impair thyroid function and may worsen hair loss
- **Ignoring gut health** — if the gut lining is compromised, nutrients from food aren't properly absorbed regardless of diet quality
- **Expecting fast results** — hair has a growth cycle of months; visible improvement from dietary changes typically takes 3–6 months, not weeks
