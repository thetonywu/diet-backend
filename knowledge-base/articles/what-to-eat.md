# What Foods Should I Eat on the Animal-Based Diet?

**Categories:** Animal-Based Diet Basics, Nutrients

## TLDR

The animal-based diet is built around meat, organ meats, eggs, fruit, honey, and raw dairy. Paul Saladino recommends prioritizing grass-fed ruminants and nose-to-tail eating while including ripe fruit and raw honey as the primary carbohydrate sources. Most vegetables, seed oils, grains, and legumes are excluded.

## Background

Many people come to the animal-based diet confused about what actually belongs on the plate — especially after years of being told to eat more vegetables and less red meat. Paul Saladino's framework inverts conventional wisdom: animal foods are the foundation, ripe fruit and honey fill carbohydrate needs, and most plant foods (especially leaves, roots, and seeds) are minimized or eliminated due to their defensive chemicals.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Meat (The Foundation)
- Prioritize **grass-fed, grass-finished ruminants**: beef, bison, lamb, goat, deer, and wild game
- Aim for roughly **1 lb of meat per 100 lbs of body weight** daily as a minimum
- 80/20 ground beef is frequently mentioned as a practical staple
- Minimize chicken and pork — monogastric animals fed corn and soy accumulate high levels of omega-6 PUFAs in their fat, which you then absorb; Saladino estimates ~99% of "pastured" chicken and pork still contains high PUFAs
- Fish in moderation: wild salmon, sardines, anchovies, and shellfish are lower-risk; avoid high-mercury species like tuna and swordfish

### Organ Meats (Critical for Nutrient Density)
- Organ meats are the most nutrient-dense foods available — Saladino calls them a cornerstone of the diet
- **Liver**: 0.5 oz daily or 2–3 oz per week (key caution: don't overdo liver due to vitamin A concentration)
- **Other organs**: heart, kidney, spleen, pancreas, brain, intestine, tripe, testicle, bone marrow, tongue
- If fresh organs are difficult to source or eat, use desiccated organ capsules — Saladino's brand **Heart & Soil** is his recommended option
- Key nutrients found in organs but not in plants: creatine, carnitine, choline, biotin, B12, taurine, vitamin K2

### Eggs
- Include eggs as a regular food
- Cook the whites slowly to neutralize avidin (which depletes biotin), then fold in raw yolks at the end — avoid fully cooking yolks to preserve fat-soluble nutrients
- Do not eat raw egg whites

### Fruit and Honey (Primary Carbohydrates)
Saladino treats ripe fruit differently from vegetables — fruit is the one plant part that evolved to be eaten (plants want seeds dispersed), so it lacks the defensive toxins found in leaves, roots, and seeds.

**Recommended fruits:** watermelon, blueberries, papaya, mango, oranges, bananas, pineapple, pears, apples, berries, dates, avocado, coconut water; fresh-squeezed juices are also included

**Carb targets by activity level** (grams per lb of goal body weight):
- Low activity (0–3 hrs/week): 0.7–0.9g
- Moderate (3–6 hrs): 0.8–1.1g
- High (6–12 hrs): 1.0–1.5g
- Very high (12+ hrs): 1.2–1.7g

**Honey:** Saladino strongly advocates raw, unfiltered honey — approximately 10 tablespoons daily in his personal diet. His brand, **Lineage Provisions**, is tested for glyphosate and never heated above 115°F.

### Raw Dairy (If Tolerated)
- Raw (unpasteurized) dairy is strongly preferred over pasteurized — Saladino argues pasteurization degrades nutrients
- Options: raw whole milk, raw kefir, raw sour cream, raw cream, raw butter, aged raw cheeses (Parmigiano-Reggiano)
- Those with lactose intolerance should try fermented raw dairy (kefir) first
- Target: ~10 oz to 1 liter of raw milk daily if it agrees with you

### Cooking Fats
- **Approved:** tallow, butter, ghee
- **Avoid all seed oils** (canola, soybean, sunflower, corn, cottonseed, safflower) — these are excluded from the entire diet, not just cooking

### Salt
- Saladino uses **Jacobsen sea salt** for its low heavy metal content

## Key Points

- The diet centers on meat, organ meats, eggs, fruit, honey, and raw dairy — animal foods provide the nutritional base; fruit and honey supply carbohydrates
- Grass-fed, grass-finished ruminants (beef, bison, lamb) are the preferred meats; chicken and pork are deprioritized due to high omega-6 content from corn/soy feed
- Organ meats are non-negotiable for Saladino — liver in particular is called the most nutrient-dense food on earth; use Heart & Soil capsules as a substitute if needed
- Ripe fruit is explicitly included; most vegetables (leaves, roots, stems) are excluded due to lectins, oxalates, isothiocyanates, and other defensive plant chemicals
- Raw honey is a significant carb source — Saladino personally eats ~10 tablespoons daily
- All seed oils are eliminated and replaced with tallow, butter, or ghee
- The animal-based diet is Saladino's evolution beyond strict carnivore — fruit and honey were added after he observed improvements in hormones, sleep, and energy

## Common Mistakes

- **Eating too much liver** — liver is high in vitamin A; Saladino cautions against eating large amounts daily. Stick to 0.5 oz per day or a few ounces per week
- **Thinking pastured chicken is fine** — most pastured chicken and pork is still corn-and-soy-fed; the monogastric metabolism means those PUFAs accumulate in the fat tissue
- **Skipping fruit and carbs** — many people go animal-based expecting to stay low-carb or keto; Saladino explicitly recommends ample carbs from fruit and honey for hormonal and metabolic health
- **Using olive oil or avocado oil liberally** — Saladino discourages excessive use of even "healthy" plant oils; tallow, butter, and ghee are preferred
- **Eating raw egg whites** — avidin in raw whites binds biotin and causes deficiency; always cook the whites
