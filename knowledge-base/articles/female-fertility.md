# How to Improve Female Fertility and Balance Hormones

**Categories:** Fertility, Women's Health, Hormones

## TLDR

Paul Saladino attributes rising female infertility and hormonal imbalances to seed oils, environmental toxins, and nutrient deficiencies. He recommends organ meats (especially liver as "nature's prenatal"), the Heart & Soil Her Package (ovary, uterus, fallopian tube, liver, kidney), adequate carbohydrates, and elimination of seed oils and endocrine disruptors to restore ovulation, progesterone production, and hormonal balance.

## Background

Female infertility has increased significantly in recent decades. Saladino argues this parallels the rise of seed oil consumption, environmental endocrine disruptors, and nutrient-poor processed diets. Conditions like PCOS — the leading cause of anovulatory infertility — are driven by insulin resistance and inflammation, both of which he traces back to seed oils and refined carbohydrates. Rather than managing symptoms with hormonal birth control, Saladino advocates addressing root causes through diet and lifestyle.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Organ meats as the foundation

- **Liver** — "nature's prenatal vitamin" providing bioavailable folate (not folic acid), retinol, B12, iron, and choline, all critical for fertility and early fetal development
- **Heart & Soil Her Package** — contains desiccated ovary, uterus, fallopian tube, liver, and kidney to provide organ-specific peptides and nutrients that support female reproductive tissue
- **Heart & Soil Firestarter** — contains thyroid and adrenal tissue; recommended for women with PCOS or sluggish thyroid function affecting fertility

### Address insulin resistance and PCOS

PCOS is fundamentally an insulin resistance condition. Seed oils and refined fructose drive insulin resistance, which elevates androgens and disrupts ovulation. Saladino's protocol:
- Eliminate all seed oils — linoleic acid accumulation in ovarian tissue is directly inflammatory
- Replace processed carbs with fruit, honey, and raw dairy
- Target fasting insulin below 5 uIU/mL
- Adequate protein (~1g per lb of lean body mass) stabilizes blood sugar

### Folate vs. folic acid

Saladino strongly distinguishes between natural folate (found in liver) and synthetic folic acid (found in supplements and fortified foods). Folic acid requires conversion by the MTHFR enzyme, and many women have polymorphisms that impair this conversion. Liver provides methylfolate directly, bypassing this issue entirely.

### Remove endocrine disruptors

- Plastics (BPA, phthalates) mimic estrogen and disrupt ovarian function
- Conventional personal care products contain parabens, triclosan, and synthetic fragrances
- Non-stick cookware (PFAS/Teflon) accumulates in ovarian tissue
- Switch to glass food storage, natural personal care products, and cast iron or stainless steel cookware

### Adequate carbohydrates for ovulation

Very low-carb diets can suppress thyroid function and raise cortisol, leading to hypothalamic amenorrhea (loss of period). Saladino recommends 150–250g of carbs daily from fruit and honey for women trying to conceive.

## Key Points

- Liver provides all the critical fertility nutrients (folate, retinol, iron, choline, B12) in bioavailable forms superior to synthetic prenatals
- PCOS is driven by insulin resistance — removing seed oils and processed carbs is the primary intervention
- Seed oils accumulate in reproductive tissues and promote inflammation that disrupts ovulation
- Very low-carb diets can cause loss of menstrual cycle by suppressing thyroid and raising cortisol
- Environmental endocrine disruptors (plastics, PFAS, parabens) directly impair ovarian function
- Natural folate from liver is preferred over folic acid due to widespread MTHFR polymorphisms

## Common Mistakes

- **Taking synthetic folic acid instead of eating liver** — many women cannot efficiently convert folic acid to active methylfolate
- **Going very low-carb while trying to conceive** — carbohydrate restriction can suppress ovulation and cause amenorrhea
- **Ignoring seed oils while focusing on supplements** — seed oil elimination is more impactful than any supplement for hormonal balance
- **Using hormonal birth control to "regulate" periods** — this masks symptoms without addressing insulin resistance or nutrient deficiencies
- **Overlooking environmental toxins** — endocrine disruptors in plastics and personal care products can undermine dietary improvements
