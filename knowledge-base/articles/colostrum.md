**Categories:** Supplements, Gut Health

## Background

Gut health is central to Saladino's animal-based framework. He argues that modern diets — particularly seed oils, gluten, lectins, and processed foods — damage the intestinal lining and create "leaky gut," allowing bacterial endotoxins (LPS) to enter circulation and drive systemic inflammation. While his primary gut-healing strategy is eliminating plant irritants and eating fermented animal foods, he positions colostrum as a targeted intervention for restoring gut barrier integrity. He's described most colostrum supplements on the market as ineffective due to poor sourcing and processing, and dedicated a full podcast episode to explaining why quality matters.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Why Colostrum Works

Bovine colostrum is not regular milk. It's produced only in the first 24-48 hours after a cow gives birth and contains a concentrated profile of bioactive compounds not found in mature milk:

- **Immunoglobulins (IgA, IgG, IgM)** — antibodies that play a direct role in immune defense and help restore gut barrier integrity by neutralizing pathogens in the intestinal lumen
- **Lactoferrin** — an iron-binding protein that starves harmful bacteria of iron while modulating immune responses; has antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties
- **Growth factors (IGF-I, IGF-II)** — insulin-like growth factors that support tissue repair, lean muscle building, nutrient metabolism, and blood sugar balance
- **Transforming Growth Factor Beta (TGF-B)** — stimulates growth and differentiation of connective tissue, supports wound healing and DNA repair
- **Lactalbumins** — proteins with demonstrated antiviral activity

### How It Fits His Gut Health Framework

Saladino recommends five interventions for gut health instead of fiber:

1. Eliminate plant irritants (lectins, oxalates, saponins, gluten, seed oils)
2. Eat fermented animal foods (raw kefir, raw milk)
3. Bone broth (1 cup/day for compromised guts — provides collagen, glycine, glutamine)
4. **Colostrum** — for immunoglobulins and bioactive proteins that restore gut barrier integrity
5. Sunlight — UVB exposure directly shifts the gut microbiome

He positions colostrum as a gut barrier support tool, not a microbiome diversity tool. The immunoglobulins repair tight junctions damaged by gluten (which triggers zonulin release), saponins, capsaicin, and lectins — preventing LPS (bacterial endotoxin) from entering circulation and causing systemic inflammation.

## Key Points

- Colostrum is the first milk produced 24-48 hours after calving — it's fundamentally different from regular milk in its bioactive profile
- The immunoglobulins (IgA, IgG, IgM) are the primary mechanism for gut barrier repair and immune support
- Lactoferrin starves harmful bacteria of iron while modulating immune responses
- Growth factors (IGF-I, IGF-II, TGF-B) support tissue repair, lean muscle, and recovery
- Colostrum addresses gut permeability (leaky gut) by restoring tight junctions damaged by gluten, lectins, and other plant compounds
- Processing method matters enormously — high-heat processing destroys the bioactive compounds; look for low-temperature spray-dried products
- Start slowly (half dose) and ramp up over weeks to avoid GI adjustment issues
- Saladino takes it daily as part of his personal supplement protocol
- Studies show measurable immune and gut benefits at 1.5-3g/day
- It's a gut barrier tool, not a probiotic — it repairs the lining rather than adding bacterial diversity

## Common Mistakes

- **Buying cheap colostrum processed with high heat** — this destroys the immunoglobulins and growth factors that make colostrum valuable; look for low-temperature, spray-dried products with third-party bioactivity testing
- **Taking full dose immediately** — colostrum is bioactive and can cause GI adjustment symptoms; ramp up from half dose over several weeks
- **Confusing colostrum with probiotics** — they serve different functions; colostrum repairs gut barrier integrity while probiotics add microbial diversity; Saladino prefers fermented foods (raw kefir) over probiotic capsules for the latter
- **Using colostrum as a substitute for dietary changes** — Saladino's gut health framework starts with eliminating plant irritants (gluten, lectins, seed oils); colostrum supports repair but doesn't override ongoing damage from a bad diet
- **Ignoring sourcing** — quality markers include grass-fed, first-milking-only collection, New Zealand or similar pasture-based sourcing, and no antibiotics or added hormones
