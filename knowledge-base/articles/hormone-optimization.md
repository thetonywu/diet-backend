# How Do I Optimize My Hormones Broadly?

**Categories:** Hormones, Nutrients

## TLDR

Paul Saladino views hormonal dysfunction — low testosterone, thyroid suppression, cortisol dysregulation, estrogen dominance — as symptoms of the same root causes: seed oil consumption, inadequate carbohydrates, nutrient deficiency, and environmental toxin exposure. His protocol addresses all hormones simultaneously through the animal-based diet (with adequate fruit and honey), organ meats, sunlight, sleep, and toxin avoidance rather than targeting individual hormones in isolation.

## Background

Hormonal complaints are among the most common reasons people seek out the animal-based diet. Men present with low testosterone, women with PCOS or menstrual irregularities, and both sexes with thyroid dysfunction, chronic fatigue, and cortisol-driven anxiety. Saladino's framework treats these as interconnected manifestations of metabolic dysfunction rather than separate conditions requiring separate interventions. His own experience — testosterone crashing to ~300 ng/dL on strict carnivore, then recovering to 857 ng/dL at age 48 after adding carbs — is his primary case study.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**The shared root causes of hormonal dysfunction:**

1. **Seed oils.** Linoleic acid drives insulin resistance, which causes obesity. Adipose tissue converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase, creating a vicious cycle. Seed oils also damage mitochondria, impairing the cellular energy production needed for hormone synthesis.
2. **Insufficient carbohydrates.** Very low-carb and ketogenic diets chronically elevate cortisol and SHBG (sex hormone-binding globulin). High SHBG binds testosterone, reducing the free (bioavailable) fraction. Cortisol directly antagonizes testosterone. Carb restriction also suppresses thyroid conversion of T4 to active T3.
3. **Nutrient deficiency.** Cholesterol is the backbone of every steroid hormone. Retinol (vitamin A from liver) is essential for testosterone production. Selenium (from kidney) is required for thyroid hormone conversion. Zinc, iodine, and B vitamins are cofactors throughout the hormonal cascade.
4. **Environmental toxins.** Plastics (BPA, phthalates), PFAS from non-stick cookware, synthetic fragrances, and fluoride in water all act as endocrine disruptors that accumulate over time.

**Hormone-specific guidance:**

*Testosterone (men and women):*
- Eat adequate carbohydrates: 150–300g/day from fruit and honey to lower SHBG and regulate cortisol
- Eat liver daily for retinol, zinc, and B12 — all required for testosterone synthesis
- Resistance training — "one of the highest-leverage testosterone interventions" (PMID 32297287)
- Morning sunlight — UVB activates a skin-brain-gonad axis that increases testosterone (PMID 34433056)
- Boron: 10mg/day if SHBG is elevated
- Heart & Soil Whole Package (testicle, liver, whole blood) for men
- Avoid TRT as a first resort — exogenous testosterone causes testes to stop producing their own

*Thyroid (T3, T4, TSH):*
- Adequate carbohydrates are critical — very low-carb diets suppress T4-to-T3 conversion, leading to functional hypothyroidism
- Selenium from kidney supports thyroid hormone conversion
- Iodine from seafood and eggs supports thyroid hormone production
- Avoid cruciferous vegetables — their goitrogens (isothiocyanates) impair thyroid function
- Avoid fluoridated water — fluoride competes with iodine at the thyroid
- Heart & Soil Firestarter (contains desiccated thyroid and adrenal tissue) for sluggish thyroid
- Target 150–250g carbs/day minimum for thyroid support

*Cortisol:*
- Chronic carb restriction is the primary dietary driver of elevated cortisol
- Fix sleep: 7–8 hours, consistent schedule, morning sunlight to set circadian rhythm, block blue light at night
- Avoid chronic endurance exercise — it elevates cortisol without the anabolic stimulus of resistance training
- Avoid OMAD and aggressive fasting — these are cortisol-elevating stressors for active people
- Eat 2–3 meals in a ~10-hour window

*Estrogen balance:*
- In men, excess estrogen comes from aromatase conversion in adipose tissue. Fix insulin resistance, lose body fat, and estrogen normalizes.
- In women, PCOS is fundamentally an insulin resistance condition. Seed oils and refined fructose drive it. Removing seed oils and eating adequate carbs from fruit and honey is the primary intervention.
- Plastics mimic estrogen — replace all plastic food storage with glass. Avoid BPA, BPS, BPE.
- Heart & Soil Her Package (ovary, uterus, fallopian tube, liver, kidney) for women's hormonal support

**Key blood work markers to track:**
- Total and free testosterone, SHBG
- Full thyroid panel: TSH, free T3, free T4
- Cortisol, DHEA-S
- Fasting insulin (target < 5 μIU/mL, ideal < 3)
- HbA1c, triglyceride-to-HDL ratio

## Key Points

- Hormonal dysfunction is not a collection of separate diseases — it's interconnected metabolic dysfunction driven by shared root causes.
- Seed oil elimination is the single most impactful intervention for hormonal health across all axes.
- Carbohydrate adequacy (150–300g/day from fruit and honey) is essential for testosterone, thyroid, and cortisol regulation. Chronic low-carb diets damage all three.
- Saladino's testosterone went from ~300 ng/dL on strict carnivore to 857 ng/dL at age 48 after adding carbs — no TRT.
- Organ meats provide the specific nutrients needed for hormone synthesis: retinol and zinc from liver, selenium from kidney, cholesterol from animal fat.
- Environmental toxin exposure (plastics, PFAS, phthalates, fluoride) accumulates and disrupts hormones even on a perfect diet.
- Cruciferous vegetables are goitrogens that impair thyroid function — Saladino recommends avoiding them entirely.
- Age-related hormonal decline is "not inevitable — it's a result of poor diet, insufficient sleep, low sun exposure, and accumulated endocrine disruptors."
- Fasting insulin below 5 μIU/mL is the most important metabolic marker. Standard lab ranges allow up to 20, which Saladino considers "dangerously loose."

## Common Mistakes

- **Treating individual hormones in isolation.** Taking thyroid medication without addressing seed oils, carb intake, and selenium deficiency misses the root cause.
- **Staying on strict keto or carnivore long-term.** Zero-carb diets suppress thyroid conversion, elevate SHBG, and raise cortisol — damaging the hormonal environment across the board.
- **Jumping to TRT or hormone replacement.** Exogenous hormones cause the body to stop producing its own. Fix diet, sleep, sunlight, and toxin exposure first.
- **Ignoring environmental endocrine disruptors.** Plastics, non-stick cookware, conventional laundry detergent, and synthetic fragrances deliver hormone-disrupting chemicals through the skin and lungs.
- **Eating cruciferous vegetables for "hormone health."** Despite their reputation, broccoli, cauliflower, kale, and Brussels sprouts contain goitrogens that impair thyroid function.
- **Under-eating carbohydrates while exercising.** This combination chronically elevates cortisol and suppresses testosterone and thyroid function simultaneously.
- **Relying on standard lab ranges.** A fasting insulin of 18 is "normal" by lab standards but signals significant metabolic dysfunction in Saladino's framework.
