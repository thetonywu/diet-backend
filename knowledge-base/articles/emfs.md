# Are EMFs Harmful? What About Wireless Headphones?

**Categories:** EMFs, Toxin Avoidance, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino warns that EMF exposure from wireless devices — especially AirPods and Bluetooth headphones worn directly against the head — is an underappreciated health risk. He recommends wired headphones, keeping phones on airplane mode when not in use, hardwiring home internet instead of using WiFi, and managing blue light exposure after sunset to protect circadian rhythm and reduce cumulative radiation exposure.

## Background

Electromagnetic fields (EMFs) from cell phones, WiFi routers, Bluetooth devices, and other wireless technology are a relatively new and constant exposure in human history. Saladino groups EMFs alongside seed oils, plastics, and environmental toxins as modern environmental stressors that our biology did not evolve to handle. While mainstream institutions generally classify non-ionizing radiation as safe at current exposure levels, Saladino and the experts he platforms argue that chronic low-level exposure — particularly from devices held against the body — may contribute to oxidative stress, disrupted sleep, and cellular damage over time.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Stop using wireless headphones

Saladino has been vocal about avoiding AirPods and Bluetooth headphones. These devices emit radiofrequency radiation directly into the ear canal and against the temporal bone, which is thin and close to the brain. He recommends:
- **Wired headphones** with a standard 3.5mm jack or Lightning/USB-C adapter
- **Air tube headphones** (wired headphones where the last segment uses an air tube instead of wire, reducing EMF reaching the ear) as the safest option
- Speakerphone when possible instead of holding the phone to the ear

### Phone habits

- Keep the phone on **airplane mode** whenever not actively using cellular or WiFi
- Never carry the phone in a front pocket — especially for men concerned about fertility
- Never sleep with the phone near the head — charge it in another room or at least across the room on airplane mode
- Use the phone on speakerphone or with wired headphones for calls

### Hardwire your home

Saladino discussed this extensively on his podcast with Ryan Blaser, an EMF specialist. The recommendation is to:
- Use ethernet cables instead of WiFi for computers and streaming devices
- Turn off the WiFi router at night (or permanently if fully hardwired)
- Hardwired connections eliminate constant WiFi radiation throughout the home

### Blue light management

While technically a different part of the electromagnetic spectrum, Saladino treats artificial blue light after sunset as part of the same EMF/modern-exposure problem:
- Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin production and disrupts circadian rhythm
- Use blue-light-blocking glasses after sunset
- Enable night mode on devices
- Prioritize morning sunlight to anchor the circadian clock

## Key Points

- AirPods and Bluetooth headphones deliver radiofrequency radiation directly against the skull — Saladino considers this an unnecessary and easily avoidable risk
- The precautionary principle applies: even if definitive long-term human studies are lacking, the cost of switching to wired headphones is zero
- Phone manufacturers include fine-print warnings about keeping devices away from the body — Saladino highlights this as tacit acknowledgment of risk
- WiFi routers emit radiation 24/7; hardwiring eliminates this constant exposure
- Blue light after sunset disrupts melatonin and sleep quality, compounding the effects of other EMF exposures
- EMF concerns are part of Saladino's broader framework of reducing modern environmental toxins that our ancestors never encountered

## Common Mistakes

- **Using AirPods for hours daily** — prolonged Bluetooth radiation directly against the head is the highest-risk personal EMF exposure
- **Sleeping with the phone on the nightstand** — hours of close-range radiation during sleep when the body should be recovering
- **Ignoring WiFi router placement** — routers in bedrooms or near sleeping areas increase nighttime exposure
- **Dismissing EMFs entirely because "the science isn't settled"** — Saladino argues the precautionary principle: low cost to reduce exposure, potentially high cost to ignore it
- **Focusing only on EMFs while ignoring diet** — EMFs are one piece of the toxin picture; seed oils and processed food are still the primary health levers
