# How to Lose Weight and Get Lean on the Animal-Based Diet

**Categories:** Animal-Based Diet Basics, Hormones

## TLDR

Paul Saladino's approach to weight loss is not about calorie restriction — it's about fixing metabolic health. He argues that seed oils and processed foods drive insulin resistance, which is the root cause of fat accumulation. By eliminating seed oils, eating nutrient-dense animal foods with ample fruit and honey, and supporting hormones through sleep, sun, and exercise, body composition normalizes naturally without counting calories.

## Background

Most conventional weight loss advice centers on calorie deficits, cardio, and willpower. Saladino takes a fundamentally different approach, arguing that obesity is a hormonal and metabolic problem — not a calorie math problem. His framework focuses on restoring insulin sensitivity, optimizing hormones (especially testosterone), and eating satiating, nutrient-dense foods that naturally regulate appetite. He is explicitly against aggressive calorie restriction, OMAD (one meal a day), and very low-carb diets for body composition goals.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**The core framework — fix the inputs, and body composition follows:**

- **Eliminate seed oils first.** This is "the most important lever to pull." Linoleic acid from seed oils accumulates in fat cells, causes them to enlarge, and drives peripheral insulin resistance. Removing seed oils is more impactful than any other single change.
- **Eliminate all processed foods, grains, and legumes.** These are secondary drivers of insulin resistance and inflammation.
- **Eat nutrient-dense animal foods ad libitum.** Grass-fed ruminant meat (~1 lb per 100 lbs body weight), organ meats (liver 0.5 oz/day), eggs, and raw dairy. Do not restrict portions.
- **Eat plenty of carbs from fruit and honey.** At least 150g/day for sedentary people, up to 300g+ for active people. Carb targets scale by activity level — 0.7–1.7g per lb body weight depending on exercise volume.
- **Do not count calories.** The diet is designed to be satiating enough through protein and fat that calorie counting is unnecessary.

**Exercise recommendations:**
- Resistance training and heavy lifting are the highest-leverage interventions for body composition and testosterone.
- Creatine monohydrate (5g/day) supports strength and lean mass (10–20% strength improvement documented).
- Colostrum for exercise recovery.

**Eating pattern:**
- 2–3 meals in a ~10-hour window. Not strict intermittent fasting.
- Finish eating 2–3 hours before bed for circadian rhythm support.
- He specifically cautions against OMAD, which can suppress testosterone and IGF-1 in active people.

**Target metabolic markers:**
- Fasting insulin: < 5 uIU/mL (optimal < 3)
- Fasting glucose: 70–85 mg/dL
- HbA1c: ≤ 5.3%
- Triglycerides: ≤ 70 mg/dL
- HDL: ≥ 60 mg/dL
- TG/HDL ratio: < 2 (optimal ~0.5)
- hsCRP: < 1 mg/L

## Key Points

- Insulin resistance, not caloric surplus, is the root cause of fat gain in Saladino's framework. Fix insulin sensitivity and body composition follows.
- Seed oil elimination is the single most impactful change for metabolic health and weight loss.
- Going too low-carb suppresses hormones, disrupts sleep, and causes muscle loss. Saladino's own testosterone dropped to ~300 ng/dL on zero-carb carnivore and recovered to 857 ng/dL after adding fruit and honey.
- Less than 1% of dietary fructose from whole fruit is converted to fat in the liver — the anti-fructose narrative is misleading when applied to whole foods.
- Obesity converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase, creating a vicious cycle that nutrient-dense eating and resistance training can break.
- Seed oil clearance from body fat takes 2–4 years, so metabolic improvements are gradual and compound over time.
- The AB30 (Animal-Based 30) challenge from Heart & Soil provides a structured 30-day entry point with food and lifestyle guidance.

## Common Mistakes

- **Going too low-carb or avoiding fruit out of sugar fear.** This suppresses thyroid, testosterone, and sleep quality, all of which impair fat loss.
- **Aggressive fasting or OMAD.** Suppresses testosterone and IGF-1 in active people, working against body composition goals.
- **Still consuming hidden seed oils.** Restaurant food is the #1 hidden source. One restaurant meal can undo days of clean eating.
- **Choosing chicken and pork over ruminant meat.** Conventionally raised poultry and pork are high in omega-6 from corn and soy feeding.
- **Skipping organ meats.** Muscle meat alone misses the most nutrient-dense foods. Use Heart & Soil supplements if whole organs aren't palatable.
- **Expecting immediate results.** Tissue-level seed oil clearance takes years. Trust the process and track metabolic markers, not just the scale.
- **Eating late at night.** Disrupts circadian rhythm and sleep, both of which are critical for fat metabolism and hormone production.
