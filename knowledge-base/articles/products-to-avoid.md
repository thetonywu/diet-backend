# Which Specific Products and Brands Should I Avoid?

**Categories:** Toxin Avoidance, Seed Oils, Budget & Practical Tips

## TLDR

Paul Saladino recommends eliminating seed oils, non-stick cookware, plastic food storage, chemical sunscreens, conventional laundry detergent, synthetic fragrances, fish oil supplements, and most processed "health foods." He names specific items to avoid and provides alternatives — cook with tallow in cast iron, store food in glass, use mineral sunscreen, and replace conventional chicken and pork with grass-fed ruminant meats.

## Background

Transitioning to an animal-based diet isn't only about what you eat — Saladino emphasizes that environmental and household exposures to endocrine disruptors, seed oils, and industrial chemicals undermine health even when diet is optimized. He frequently covers "kitchen detox" and product swaps in his newsletters and YouTube content, arguing that the cumulative load of plastics, PFAS, phthalates, and seed oils is a major driver of hormonal disruption and chronic disease.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Cooking oils to eliminate

The "hateful eight" seed oils — remove all of these from your kitchen:

| Eliminate | Replace with |
|---|---|
| Canola oil | Tallow |
| Soybean oil | Butter |
| Corn oil | Ghee |
| Sunflower oil | Lard (from pasture-raised pork) |
| Safflower oil | Coconut oil |
| Cottonseed oil | — |
| Grapeseed oil | — |
| Rice bran oil | — |

Commercial olive oil and avocado oil are frequently adulterated with seed oils. Only buy from reputable sources and verify purity.

### Food products to avoid

- **Conventional chicken and pork** — ~99% of "pastured" poultry/pork still has high linoleic acid from corn/soy feed; prefer grass-fed ruminants (beef, bison, lamb)
- **American cheese** — the FDA doesn't classify it as real cheese (contains <50% actual cheese); also contains microplastics
- **Pasteurized, homogenized A1 dairy** — Saladino calls pasteurized milk "dead milk"; choose raw dairy from A2 breeds when possible
- **Pasteurized/filtered honey** — processing destroys enzymes and antioxidants; buy raw, unfiltered honey
- **High-fructose corn syrup** — in any form, avoid at all costs
- **Nightshade "superfoods"** — tomatoes, peppers, goji berries contain lectins and solanine
- **"Healthy" plant foods marketed as superfoods** — kale, spinach, beets, turmeric contain oxalates, lectins, or other plant defense chemicals

### Cookware and kitchen items

- **Non-stick pans (Teflon/PFAS)** — linked to hormonal disruption and reduced sperm count; replace with cast iron, stainless steel, or carbon steel
- **Plastic food storage containers** — BPA, BPS, and BPE disrupt hormone signaling; "BPA-free" products often substitute equally harmful chemicals; use glass or stainless steel containers
- **Plastic water bottles** — source of microplastics; use glass or stainless steel bottles
- **Plastic cutting boards** — shed microplastics into food; use wood

### Personal care and household

- **Chemical sunscreens** — oxybenzone, avobenzone, and octinoxate are hormone disruptors absorbed through the skin; use tallow-based zinc oxide sunscreen instead
- **Conventional laundry detergent** — many contain phthalates that absorb through skin via bedding during sleep
- **Synthetic fragrances** (perfumes, air fresheners, scented candles) — contain phthalates that act as endocrine disruptors
- **Polyester underwear** — Saladino cites a study (PMID 8279095) showing polyester next to the testes disrupted sperm production; wear cotton or wool

### Supplements to avoid

- **Fish oil** — 83% of products tested exceed recommended peroxide levels; only 8% meet international standards; linked to increased atrial fibrillation risk
- **Multivitamins** — poor bioavailability forms with industrial fillers (silicon dioxide, polyvinyl pyrrolidone); unnecessary when eating organs
- **Methylene blue** — inhibits MAO-A, decreases ATP in healthy mitochondria, can cause serotonin syndrome
- **Melatonin supplements** — support natural production through light management instead
- **Magnesium oxide** — poorly absorbed; primarily acts as a laxative; use glycinate or threonate
- **Creatine HCl or buffered creatine** — more expensive with no proven advantage over monohydrate

## Key Points

- Seed oils are hidden in nearly all packaged and restaurant food — label reading is essential; even "healthy" products like salad dressings, granola, and bread typically contain them
- "BPA-free" plastics are not safe — the replacement chemicals BPS and BPE may be equally or more harmful as endocrine disruptors
- Conventional chicken is one of the largest sources of dietary linoleic acid, yet is widely perceived as a healthy protein choice
- Non-stick cookware releases PFAS ("forever chemicals") that accumulate in the body and are linked to hormonal disruption
- Most commercial fish oil is already oxidized by the time it reaches the consumer, making it potentially pro-inflammatory rather than anti-inflammatory
- Phthalates from laundry detergent and synthetic fragrances are absorbed through the skin during sleep — a significant exposure window most people overlook
- Fluoridated tap water may disrupt the pineal gland; Saladino recommends filtered water or tested mineral water brands

## Common Mistakes

- **Only cleaning up diet while ignoring household products** — endocrine disruptors from cookware, detergent, and personal care accumulate and undermine hormonal health even on a perfect diet
- **Trusting "natural" or "organic" labels on processed foods** — many organic packaged products still contain seed oils
- **Switching to "BPA-free" plastic and assuming it's safe** — the replacement chemicals have similar endocrine-disrupting properties
- **Buying commercial olive oil without verifying purity** — many brands are adulterated with cheaper seed oils
- **Taking fish oil because "omega-3s are healthy"** — the oxidation and rancidity of most products may cause more harm than benefit; eat wild-caught fatty fish instead
- **Replacing beef with chicken for "heart health"** — conventional chicken fat is very high in linoleic acid, which Saladino considers the actual cardiovascular risk factor
