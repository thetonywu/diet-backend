# Which Specific Products and Brands Should I Avoid?

**Categories:** Toxin Avoidance, Seed Oils, Budget & Practical Tips

## TLDR

Paul Saladino recommends eliminating seed oils, non-stick cookware, plastic food storage, chemical sunscreens, conventional laundry detergent, synthetic fragrances, fish oil supplements, and most processed "health foods." He names specific items to avoid and provides alternatives — cook with tallow in cast iron, store food in glass, use mineral sunscreen, and replace conventional chicken and pork with grass-fed ruminant meats.

## Background

Transitioning to an animal-based diet isn't only about what you eat — Saladino emphasizes that household exposures to endocrine disruptors and industrial chemicals undermine health even when diet is optimized. He argues that the cumulative load of plastics, PFAS, phthalates, and seed oils is a major driver of hormonal disruption and chronic disease.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Cooking oils to eliminate

The "hateful eight" seed oils — remove all from your kitchen: canola, soybean, corn, sunflower, safflower, cottonseed, grapeseed, and rice bran oil. Replace with tallow, butter, ghee, pasture-raised lard, or coconut oil.

Commercial olive oil and avocado oil are frequently adulterated with seed oils. Only buy from reputable sources.

### Food products to avoid

- **Conventional chicken and pork** — high linoleic acid from corn/soy feed; prefer grass-fed ruminants
- **American cheese** — FDA doesn't classify it as real cheese; also contains microplastics
- **Pasteurized, homogenized A1 dairy** — choose raw dairy from A2 breeds when possible
- **Pasteurized/filtered honey** — buy raw, unfiltered honey instead
- **High-fructose corn syrup** — in any form, avoid at all costs
- **Nightshade "superfoods"** — tomatoes, peppers, goji berries contain lectins and solanine

### Cookware and kitchen items

- **Non-stick pans (Teflon/PFAS)** — linked to hormonal disruption; replace with cast iron, stainless steel, or carbon steel
- **Plastic food storage** — BPA, BPS, and BPE disrupt hormones; "BPA-free" often substitutes equally harmful chemicals; use glass or stainless steel
- **Plastic water bottles** — source of microplastics; use glass or stainless steel

### Personal care and household

- **Chemical sunscreens** — oxybenzone, avobenzone, octinoxate are hormone disruptors; use tallow-based zinc oxide instead
- **Conventional laundry detergent** — many contain phthalates absorbed through skin via bedding
- **Synthetic fragrances** — contain phthalates that act as endocrine disruptors
- **Polyester underwear** — Saladino cites a study ([PMID 8279095](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8279095/)) showing polyester next to the testes disrupted sperm production; wear cotton or wool

### Supplements to avoid

- **Fish oil** — 83% of products exceed recommended peroxide levels; linked to atrial fibrillation risk
- **Multivitamins** — poor bioavailability with industrial fillers; unnecessary when eating organs
- **Methylene blue** — inhibits MAO-A, decreases ATP in healthy mitochondria
- **Melatonin supplements** — support natural production through light management instead
- **Magnesium oxide** — poorly absorbed; use glycinate or threonate
- **Creatine HCl or buffered creatine** — no advantage over monohydrate

## Key Points

- Seed oils are hidden in nearly all packaged and restaurant food — label reading is essential
- "BPA-free" plastics are not safe — replacement chemicals BPS and BPE may be equally harmful
- Conventional chicken is one of the largest sources of dietary linoleic acid, yet is widely perceived as healthy
- Non-stick cookware releases PFAS ("forever chemicals") that accumulate in the body
- Most commercial fish oil is already oxidized, making it potentially pro-inflammatory
- Phthalates from laundry detergent and fragrances absorb through the skin during sleep
- Fluoridated tap water may disrupt the pineal gland; Saladino recommends filtered or mineral water

## Common Mistakes

- **Only cleaning up diet while ignoring household products** — endocrine disruptors from cookware and personal care undermine health even on a perfect diet
- **Trusting "natural" or "organic" labels** — many organic packaged products still contain seed oils
- **Switching to "BPA-free" plastic** — replacement chemicals have similar endocrine-disrupting properties
- **Taking fish oil for omega-3s** — oxidation and rancidity of most products may cause more harm than benefit; eat wild-caught fatty fish instead
- **Replacing beef with chicken for "heart health"** — conventional chicken fat is very high in linoleic acid
