# How Do I Detox My Kitchen and Home?

**Categories:** Toxin Avoidance, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino argues that household toxin exposure undermines health even on a perfect diet. His detox framework covers cookware (replace non-stick with cast iron or stainless steel), food storage (glass instead of plastic), cleaning products (baking soda and white vinegar only), laundry (white vinegar, no conventional detergent), and personal care (water only, beef tallow as moisturizer). The goal is to eliminate PFAS, phthalates, parabens, BPA, and synthetic fragrances from your living environment.

## Background

Modern homes contain hundreds of synthetic chemicals that did not exist a century ago. Saladino considers this accumulated chemical exposure — from cookware coatings to laundry detergent to plastic food containers — a significant and underappreciated driver of metabolic dysfunction, hormonal disruption, and chronic disease. He argues that cleaning up your environment is a necessary complement to cleaning up your diet, not an optional extra.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

Saladino's home detox approach is organized room by room, with the kitchen as the highest-priority target.

**Kitchen:**
- Replace all non-stick pans (Teflon). Non-stick coatings contain PFAS ("forever chemicals") that aerosolize when heated and are linked to hormonal disruption and cancer. Switch to cast iron, stainless steel, or carbon steel cookware.
- Replace all plastic food storage containers with glass. "BPA-free" plastics often contain BPS or BPE, which may be equally harmful endocrine disruptors.
- Use wooden cutting boards instead of plastic.
- Eliminate all seed oils from the pantry. Cook with tallow, butter, ghee, or coconut oil.
- Check all packaged food labels for hidden seed oils, HFCS, and artificial additives.
- Saladino brings his own stainless steel pan when traveling to avoid hotel non-stick cookware.

**Laundry and bedding:**
- Replace conventional laundry detergent with white vinegar. New York State banned popular brands including Arm & Hammer Clean Burst and Tide Original for containing 1,4-dioxane, a probable carcinogen.
- Use 100% cotton or wool bedding. Synthetic fabrics contain chemicals that absorb through the skin during 8 hours of sleep.
- Avoid fabric softeners and dryer sheets containing synthetic fragrances.

**Cleaning products:**
- Replace all chemical cleaners with baking soda and white vinegar.
- Eliminate air fresheners, scented candles, and plug-in fragrances. Companies hide dozens of toxic chemicals under the single word "fragrance" on labels.

**Bathroom:**
- Toilet paper may contain BPA — consider unbleached alternatives.
- Eliminate conventional toothpaste (fluoride disrupts thyroid function and may calcify the pineal gland). Use a natural boar's hair toothbrush with water only.
- Eliminate mouthwash — it kills beneficial oral bacteria responsible for nitric oxide production.
- Use beef tallow as moisturizer instead of petroleum-based lotions.

**Clothing:**
- Wear 100% cotton or wool. Avoid polyester, especially underwear — research (PMID 8279095) shows polyester next to testes disrupted sperm production.

**Water:**
- Install a reverse osmosis filtration system or drink spring water from glass bottles.
- Avoid plastic water bottles — microplastics and endocrine disruptors leach into the water, especially when heated.
- Avoid fluoridated tap water.

## Key Points

- Non-stick cookware (Teflon) aerosolizes PFAS when heated. Cast iron, stainless steel, and carbon steel are the safe alternatives.
- "BPA-free" plastic is not safe — BPS and BPE replacements are also endocrine disruptors. Glass is the only reliable alternative for food storage.
- Conventional laundry detergent is a major source of phthalate exposure, absorbed through skin during sleep via bedding and clothing.
- The word "fragrance" on any label can hide dozens of undisclosed toxic chemicals including phthalates.
- The average woman uses 12 personal care products daily, exposing herself to over 160 unique chemicals.
- PFAS are called "forever chemicals" because they do not break down in the environment or the human body.
- Household chemical exposure accumulates and compounds with dietary toxins — cleaning up only diet while ignoring the home environment leaves significant toxin exposure unaddressed.
- Saladino's philosophy is elimination, not substitution. Rather than switching to "cleaner" commercial products, he removes most products entirely.

## Common Mistakes

- **Thinking "BPA-free" plastic is safe.** Replacement chemicals (BPS, BPE) are potentially equally harmful endocrine disruptors. Switch to glass entirely.
- **Focusing only on diet and ignoring the home environment.** Toxins from cookware, cleaning products, and personal care products enter the body through skin absorption and inhalation, bypassing the gut entirely.
- **Replacing chemical cleaners with "natural" branded products without reading labels.** Many "green" cleaners still contain synthetic fragrances and other problematic chemicals.
- **Keeping old non-stick pans "just for eggs."** Any heating of PFAS-coated cookware releases toxic compounds. Replace them all.
- **Ignoring laundry detergent.** This is one of the most overlooked toxin sources — you sleep in sheets washed with phthalate-containing detergent for 8 hours every night.
- **Underestimating clothing materials.** Synthetic fabrics in direct skin contact (especially underwear) deliver chemicals transdermally throughout the day.
