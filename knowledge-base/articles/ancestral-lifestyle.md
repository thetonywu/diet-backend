# How Do I Live a More Ancestral Lifestyle?

**Categories:** Ancestral Lifestyle, The Sun

## TLDR

Paul Saladino's ancestral lifestyle framework goes beyond diet to include daily sunlight exposure, circadian-aligned sleep, outdoor movement, grounding (barefoot earth contact), cold and heat exposure, EMF reduction, and systematic toxin elimination. The core principle is that human biology evolved over millions of years in specific environmental conditions, and modern deviations from those conditions — artificial light, indoor living, processed food, chemical exposure — are the root cause of chronic disease and declining health.

## Background

The animal-based diet is only one component of Saladino's broader philosophy. He argues that even a perfect diet cannot fully compensate for a lifestyle that violates evolutionary biology — spending all day indoors under artificial light, sleeping irregularly, never touching the earth, and living in a chemical-laden environment. The ancestral lifestyle model seeks to realign daily habits with the conditions under which human physiology developed, using modern knowledge to approximate what was once the default human experience.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**Sunlight and circadian rhythm (his most emphasized lifestyle factor):**
- Get direct morning sunlight within the first hour of waking. This sets the circadian clock and initiates cortisol, serotonin, and vitamin D pathways.
- UVB exposure activates a skin-brain-gonad axis that increases testosterone and libido (Cell Reports 2021, PMID 34433056).
- Sunlight triggers nitric oxide synthesis in the skin, which supports blood vessel dilation and blood pressure regulation.
- Sunlight exposure shifts the gut microbiome composition — a benefit that cannot be replicated by vitamin D supplementation alone.
- Avoid chemical sunscreens. Use zinc oxide mineral sunscreen only for extended exposure. Eliminating seed oils from the diet reduces sunburn susceptibility.
- Use the D-Minder app to track personal UV exposure.

**Sleep aligned with natural light cycles:**
- 7–8 hours at consistent times.
- Block blue light 1–2 hours before bed (blue-light-blocking glasses, dimmed screens).
- Sleep in complete darkness.
- Morning sunlight exposure is the most important circadian signal.
- Finish eating 2–3 hours before bed.
- Glycine from bone broth and connective tissue promotes deep sleep.
- Carbohydrates from fruit and honey support the serotonin-to-melatonin conversion pathway. Do not restrict carbs.
- Avoid supplemental melatonin — support the body's own production through light management.

**Outdoor movement and nature connection:**
- Exercise outdoors whenever possible. Saladino surfs daily and considers the combination of sunlight, movement, and nature exposure multiplicative.
- Daily walking: two or three 15-minute walks around meals for blood glucose control.
- Resistance training as the primary exercise modality.
- Hiking, swimming, gardening, and fishing as regular activities.
- Avoid chronic indoor endurance exercise (treadmill running) — it removes the sunlight and nature exposure benefits while adding stress.

**Grounding (earthing):**
- Bare skin contact with the earth (grass, soil, sand, water). Saladino recommends trying it, though with less certainty than diet, sunlight, and sleep interventions.
- Walk barefoot outdoors when practical.

**Heat and cold exposure:**
- Sauna use for detoxification and immune function. Heat stress triggers heat shock proteins that support cellular repair.
- Cold exposure (cold plunge, cold shower) for fat metabolism, vascular health, and norepinephrine release.
- These are supplementary practices, not replacements for diet and sleep fundamentals.

**EMF reduction:**
- Use wired or air tube headphones instead of AirPods or Bluetooth.
- Put phone on airplane mode when not in use and never carry it in a front pocket.
- Hardwire home internet connections. Turn off WiFi router at night.
- Minimize screen time, especially after sunset.

**Toxin elimination from the home environment:**
- Replace non-stick cookware with cast iron or stainless steel.
- Replace plastic food storage with glass.
- Use white vinegar for laundry, baking soda and vinegar for cleaning.
- Eliminate synthetic fragrances, air fresheners, and scented candles.
- Wear 100% cotton or wool clothing.
- Filter water (reverse osmosis) or drink spring water from glass bottles.
- Use water only for hair and body. Beef tallow as moisturizer.

**Social connection and stress management:**
- Engage in meditation, prayer, gardening, or nature connection activities.
- The AB30 (Animal-Based 30) program includes community elements — weekly group workouts and shared challenges.
- Saladino emphasizes reducing sedentary behavior, noting that modern adults sit 8+ hours daily.

## Key Points

- The ancestral lifestyle is a unified framework — diet, sunlight, sleep, movement, grounding, temperature exposure, and toxin avoidance work together. Optimizing one while ignoring others limits results.
- Morning sunlight is the single most important non-dietary intervention. It sets circadian rhythm, triggers hormone production, synthesizes vitamin D, and shifts the gut microbiome.
- Modern indoor living is fundamentally misaligned with human biology. Artificial light, temperature-controlled environments, and sedentary behavior create the conditions for chronic disease.
- EMFs are "modern environmental stressors that our biology did not evolve to handle" — the precautionary principle applies.
- The shift from ancestral omega-6:omega-3 ratios (~1:1) to modern ratios (20:1) from seed oils is one of the largest and most consequential dietary changes in human history.
- Ancestral populations consumed ~2–3% of calories from linoleic acid versus ~20% in modern Americans — a 10-fold increase that Saladino considers the primary driver of the chronic disease epidemic.
- Age-related decline — in hormones, energy, cognitive function, and physical capacity — is "not inevitable" but a consequence of modern lifestyle misalignment. Saladino cites his own health markers at 48 as evidence.
- The ancestral model is not about recreating primitive conditions. It is about understanding evolutionary biology and applying those principles with modern knowledge and tools.

## Common Mistakes

- **Optimizing diet while ignoring sunlight and sleep.** A perfect diet eaten indoors under artificial light with poor sleep will produce suboptimal results.
- **Wearing sunscreen every day.** Chemical sunscreens block beneficial UVB rays needed for vitamin D, nitric oxide, and hormonal production while delivering endocrine-disrupting chemicals through the skin.
- **Using screens until bedtime.** Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin and disrupts the circadian clock. Block blue light 1–2 hours before bed.
- **Exercising exclusively indoors.** Gym workouts miss the sunlight, fresh air, and nature exposure that outdoor exercise provides. Move outside whenever possible.
- **Dismissing grounding and temperature exposure as fringe.** While Saladino assigns less certainty to grounding than to diet and sunlight, sauna and cold exposure have meaningful evidence for immune and vascular benefits.
- **Attempting to optimize with supplements and biohacks before fixing the basics.** Sunlight, sleep, diet, and movement are the foundation. Supplements and devices are marginal additions, not replacements.
- **Thinking the ancestral lifestyle requires extreme measures.** Walking outside in the morning, eating real food, sleeping in the dark, and using glass instead of plastic are simple, practical changes — not radical lifestyle overhauls.
