# How to Boost Testosterone Naturally on an Animal-Based Diet

**Categories:** Hormones, Nutrients, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino argues that declining testosterone is not inevitable — it's a result of poor diet, insufficient sleep, low sun exposure, and accumulated endocrine disruptors. He maintains testosterone of 857 ng/dL at age 48 without TRT by eating nose-to-tail animal foods with ample fruit and honey, prioritizing sleep and sunlight, and eliminating seed oils and environmental toxins.

## Background

Men's testosterone levels have dropped 30–40% over the last 40–50 years. Low testosterone is increasingly common even in younger men, and many turn to TRT as a first solution. Saladino argues this treats the symptom while ignoring the root cause — and that the body's own production can be restored or dramatically improved through diet, lifestyle, and toxin removal.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### His personal story
Saladino's own hormone journey is central to his credibility on this topic. On strict carnivore (zero carbs), his testosterone dropped to ~300 ng/dL — the bottom of the reference range — while his SHBG spiked to 103–120 (normal: 16–55). After adding ~300g of carbohydrates daily from fruit and honey, his testosterone rose to 857 ng/dL at age 48 with no TRT.

His explanation: very low-carb diets chronically raise SHBG, which binds testosterone and reduces the *free* (bioavailable) fraction. Carbohydrates from fruit and honey lower SHBG and help regulate cortisol — both of which liberate more testosterone.

### Dietary recommendations

**Eat these foods for testosterone support:**
- **Liver** (3–5 oz weekly): provides zinc, retinol (vitamin A), copper, and B vitamins — all required for testosterone synthesis. Saladino calls retinol "absolutely essential for testosterone production."
- **Beef testicle**: contains activin, inhibin, epidermal growth factor, selenium, and zinc — tissue-specific compounds that directly support testosterone production. The principle he cites: "like supports like."
- **Kidney**: selenium and B vitamins support thyroid function, which in turn supports testosterone
- **Heart**: CoQ10 for cellular energy and hormone synthesis
- **Grass-fed beef**: saturated fats (stearic acid, C15 fatty acid) serve as the building blocks for steroid hormones — cholesterol is the backbone of every hormone in the body
- **Raw dairy**: fat-soluble vitamins A, D, K2 plus saturated fat as hormone precursors; ~1 liter of raw milk daily in his own diet
- **Fruit and honey**: carbohydrates to lower SHBG and keep cortisol in check; his own daily intake includes ~550g blueberries, 250g watermelon, 250g papaya, and ~10 tablespoons of raw honey

**Carbohydrate targets by activity level** (grams per lb of goal body weight, from fruit and honey):
- Low activity (0–3 hrs/week): 0.7–0.9g/lb
- Moderate (3–6 hrs/week): 0.8–1.1g/lb
- High (6–12 hrs/week): 1.0–1.5g/lb
- Very high (12+ hrs/week): 1.2–1.7g/lb (Saladino's own intake is ~300g/day)

**Key nutrients for testosterone:**
- Zinc (liver, red meat)
- Retinol / vitamin A (liver, raw dairy)
- Selenium (kidney, testicle)
- Cholesterol (all animal fats)
- C15 fatty acid (grass-fed dairy)
- Boron — he personally supplemented 10 mg/day to address elevated SHBG

### Lifestyle recommendations

- **Sleep 7–8 hours at consistent times** — non-negotiable; the brain releases LH (luteinizing hormone) during sleep to signal the testes to produce testosterone. Chronic sleep disruption directly suppresses T.
- **Daily sunlight exposure** — Saladino cites a 2021 Cell Reports study (PMID 34433056) showing UVB light activates a skin-brain-gonad axis, increasing testosterone and libido. He recommends avoiding sunscreen to allow direct UV exposure.
- **Resistance training** — heavy lifting is one of the highest-leverage testosterone interventions; associated with higher testosterone in studies he cites (PMID 32297287)
- **Reduce chronic stress** — elevated cortisol is directly antagonistic to testosterone; he notes that strict carnivore/keto can chronically raise cortisol if carbohydrates are too low
- **Grounding** — bare skin contact with earth; he recommends trying it, though with less certainty than the above interventions
- **Blue light management** — block blue light after sunset; use incandescent lighting; turn off WiFi at night

### What lowers testosterone — eliminate these

**Dietary:**
- All seed oils — Saladino claims they contain phthalates (endocrine disruptors) and drive insulin resistance, both of which suppress testosterone
- Processed foods and refined carbohydrates — drive insulin resistance
- Long-term strict low-carb / carnivore without fruit and honey — raises SHBG, suppresses free testosterone, and chronically elevates cortisol
- Alcohol and marijuana — suppress sleep quality and testosterone directly

**Environmental endocrine disruptors:**
- **Plastic bottles and food storage** — BPA, BPS, and BPE all disrupt hormone signaling; "BPA-free" often means BPS or BPE instead, which may be worse
- **Non-stick cookware (PFAS/Teflon)** — linked to reduced sperm count and hormonal disruption
- **Conventional laundry detergent** — many contain phthalates absorbed through skin via bedding during sleep
- **Synthetic fragrances** — contain phthalates
- **Polyester underwear** — he cites a study (PMID 8279095) showing polyester next to the testes caused significant disruption to sperm production; wear cotton or wool
- **Fluoride** — he links it to potential pineal gland disruption

**Metabolic:**
- Fasting insulin above 5 μIU/mL — signals metabolic dysfunction that depresses testosterone; Saladino considers the standard reference range (up to 20 μIU/mL) dangerously loose
- Obesity — adipose tissue converts testosterone to estrogen via aromatase

### His position on TRT
Saladino is strongly opposed to TRT as a first resort:
- Exogenous testosterone tells the brain to stop signaling the testes to produce their own — the testes go into "hibernation"
- After years of high-dose TRT, it becomes very difficult to restore natural production
- His recommendation: fix insulin resistance, diet quality, sleep, sunlight, and toxin exposure first; in his view, most men in their 40s–60s don't need TRT at all

## Key Points

- Very low-carb and strict carnivore diets raise SHBG, which binds testosterone and reduces the bioavailable free fraction — adding fruit and honey is key to releasing it
- Cholesterol from animal fat is the precursor to all steroid hormones; eating saturated fat supports, not harms, testosterone production
- Organ meats — especially liver, testicle, and kidney — provide the specific micronutrients (zinc, retinol, selenium) required for testosterone synthesis
- Sleep and sunlight are physiological triggers for testosterone production, not optional extras; UVB light activates a skin-brain-gonad hormonal axis
- Endocrine disruptors from plastics, non-stick cookware, synthetic detergents, and seed oils cumulatively suppress testosterone over time
- Saladino went from ~300 ng/dL on strict carnivore to 857 ng/dL at age 48 on animal-based diet with fruit and honey, without TRT
- TRT suppresses the body's own production and creates dependency — he recommends exhausting natural optimization first

## Common Mistakes

- **Going strict carnivore (zero carbs) for hormone optimization** — Saladino's own experience showed this tanks testosterone via elevated SHBG; fruit and honey are necessary
- **Focusing only on total testosterone** — SHBG-bound T is not usable; free testosterone and SHBG levels matter as much as the total number
- **Ignoring environmental toxins** — diet alone won't overcome constant phthalate and PFAS exposure from cookware, plastics, and laundry products
- **Skipping sunlight or using sunscreen routinely** — blocking UV blocks a documented hormonal signaling pathway
- **Jumping to TRT before trying dietary/lifestyle optimization** — Saladino argues this is usually unnecessary and creates long-term dependency
