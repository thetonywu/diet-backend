# How Do I Improve Flexibility and Mobility on the Animal-Based Diet?

**Categories:** Fitness, Nutrients

## TLDR

Paul Saladino treats joint stiffness, pain, and poor mobility as symptoms of chronic inflammation and connective tissue degradation driven by seed oils, plant toxins, and nutrient deficiency — not as inevitable consequences of aging. His protocol centers on eliminating inflammatory triggers, eating collagen-rich foods (bone broth, bone marrow, cartilage), and providing the raw materials for connective tissue repair through organ meats and targeted supplements like Heart & Soil Bone Matrix.

## Background

Many people who switch to the animal-based diet report unexpected improvements in joint pain, stiffness, and range of motion. Saladino attributes this to the removal of inflammatory dietary triggers (seed oils and plant defense chemicals like lectins and oxalates) combined with increased intake of collagen, glycine, and fat-soluble nutrients that support connective tissue repair. His framework treats mobility as a downstream indicator of metabolic and inflammatory health rather than a standalone fitness goal.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

**Step 1: Remove the inflammatory triggers**
- Eliminate seed oils. Linoleic acid drives systemic inflammation through OXLAMs (oxidized linoleic acid metabolites), which damage mitochondria and trigger immune responses that manifest as joint pain and stiffness.
- Eliminate plant defense chemicals. Lectins (grains, legumes, nightshades), oxalates (spinach, almonds, turmeric), and gluten all damage the gut lining and can trigger autoimmune joint inflammation. Joint pain is explicitly listed alongside eczema and psoriasis as an autoimmune condition driven by gut permeability.
- Avoid NSAIDs for pain management. They treat symptoms without addressing root causes, and chronic use damages the gut lining further.
- Avoid turmeric/curcumin supplements. Despite their "anti-inflammatory" reputation, turmeric is high in oxalates — a plant toxin that contributes to kidney stones and mineral depletion.

**Step 2: Provide the raw materials for connective tissue repair**
- Bone broth (1 cup/day): provides glycine, collagen, and glutamine to support the gut lining and connective tissue regeneration.
- Bone marrow: rich in collagen, fat-soluble vitamins, and stem cell factors that support connective tissue.
- Cartilage and connective tissue from bone-in meats: gelatin from these sources directly supports joint structure.
- Fruit (berries, citrus, papaya): vitamin C is required for collagen synthesis.
- Liver: retinol (vitamin A) is essential for tissue repair and immune regulation.

**Step 3: Heal the gut**
- Colostrum (Heart & Soil, 3g powder or 6 capsules/day): contains TGF-B (Transforming Growth Factor Beta), which stimulates growth and differentiation of connective tissue and supports wound healing. Also contains IGF-I and IGF-II for tissue repair.
- Removing plant toxins and seed oils allows tight junctions in the gut lining to heal, reducing systemic inflammation that drives joint symptoms.

**Step 4: Targeted supplements**
- Heart & Soil Bone Matrix — long bones, bone marrow, and cartilage for joint and bone density support.
- Heart & Soil Joint Strength & Repair — trachea and scapula cartilage.
- Heart & Soil Skin, Hair & Nails — contains full-spectrum collagen (Types I–V) and glycosaminoglycans from grass-fed cartilage.
- Magnesium glycinate or threonate (200–400mg/day) — supports muscle relaxation and recovery.

**Step 5: Movement**
- Resistance training to strengthen muscles that support joints.
- Daily walking (two or three 15-minute walks) for blood flow and gentle joint mobilization.
- Outdoor activity for sunlight benefits — vitamin D supports bone and joint health.
- Avoid chronic endurance exercise, which creates repetitive stress on joints without adequate recovery.

## Key Points

- Joint pain, stiffness, and poor mobility are symptoms of chronic inflammation and gut permeability — not inevitable aging.
- Saladino experienced joint pain on strict zero-carb carnivore, which resolved after adding ~300g/day of fruit and honey. Adequate carbohydrates reduce cortisol, which can exacerbate inflammatory joint conditions.
- Autoimmune joint conditions (rheumatoid arthritis, reactive arthritis) are driven by gut permeability from plant toxins and seed oils — they often resolve when dietary triggers are removed.
- Bone broth is the primary food source for collagen, glycine, and glutamine — all essential for connective tissue repair.
- Colostrum contains growth factors (TGF-B, IGF-I, IGF-II) that directly stimulate connective tissue growth and repair.
- Oxalates from spinach, almonds, and turmeric can deposit in joints and contribute to pain. Eliminating high-oxalate foods is part of the protocol.
- Fish oil, commonly recommended for joint inflammation, is typically oxidized and may be pro-inflammatory. Whole fatty fish is the safer alternative.
- Most people notice significant improvement in joint symptoms within 30–90 days of eliminating seed oils and plant toxins.

## Common Mistakes

- **Taking turmeric or curcumin for joint pain.** Turmeric is high in oxalates, which can actually worsen joint issues by depositing in tissues.
- **Using NSAIDs chronically.** They mask symptoms while further damaging the gut lining, which is often the root cause of joint inflammation.
- **Supplementing with fish oil for joints.** Most commercial fish oil is oxidized beyond safe limits and may increase inflammation rather than reduce it.
- **Ignoring the gut connection.** Joint pain is often an autoimmune manifestation of leaky gut. Treating joints without healing the gut misses the root cause.
- **Staying on strict zero-carb carnivore.** Saladino's own joint pain on carnivore resolved after adding carbs. Chronic carb restriction elevates cortisol, which can worsen inflammatory conditions.
- **Expecting topical treatments to solve systemic inflammation.** Joint health is built from the inside out through nutrition, not through creams, patches, or external devices.
