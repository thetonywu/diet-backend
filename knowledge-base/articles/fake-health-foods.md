# What Are "Fake Health Foods" I Should Watch Out For?

**Categories:** Toxin Avoidance, Seed Oils

## TLDR

Many foods marketed as "healthy" are anything but, according to Saladino. The biggest offenders are products that hide seed oils behind clean-sounding labels — keto snacks, protein bars, organic packaged foods, commercial olive oil, and plant-based meat alternatives. His rule of thumb: if it comes in a package with a health claim, it's probably full of seed oils, plant toxins, or both.

## Background

The modern health food industry has created an enormous market of products that use words like "natural," "organic," "plant-based," and "superfood" to appear healthy while containing the exact ingredients Saladino considers most harmful — seed oils, refined sweeteners, and plant defense chemicals. For people transitioning to an animal-based diet, learning to see through this marketing is critical, because many of these products are staples of the standard "health-conscious" diet.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

Saladino's approach is simple: eat real, whole animal foods and stop trusting labels. Here are the specific categories of fake health foods he calls out:

**"Keto" processed foods** — Many keto bars, snacks, and dressings contain seed oils, meaning people restrict carbs while still consuming the fat Saladino considers the actual driver of insulin resistance and metabolic disease. The marketing focuses on low-carb counts while hiding canola or sunflower oil in the ingredients.

**Conventional chicken as "lean protein"** — Mainstream nutrition promotes chicken breast as the healthy meat, but conventionally raised chicken is one of the largest sources of dietary linoleic acid. The birds are fed corn and soy, and the omega-6 fat concentrates in their tissue. Saladino considers switching from beef to chicken a step backward.

**Pasteurized yogurt as "probiotic food"** — Store-bought pasteurized yogurt contains far fewer live cultures and no active enzymes compared to raw milk kefir. The probiotic claims on commercial yogurt are largely marketing.

**Fish oil supplements** — Marketed as heart-healthy, but 83% of commercial fish oil products exceed recommended peroxide levels, meaning they're already oxidized (rancid) before you buy them. Oxidized omega-3 PUFAs may actually increase inflammation. Some studies link regular fish oil use to increased atrial fibrillation and stroke risk.

**Turmeric and curcumin supplements** — Sold as "anti-inflammatory," but turmeric is high in oxalates, which Saladino considers a plant toxin. His approach to inflammation is to remove the causes (seed oils, plant toxins) rather than try to counteract them with more plant chemicals.

**"Anti-inflammatory" salads and leafy greens** — Spinach, kale, and chard are marketed as superfoods but contain oxalates that bind calcium, contribute to kidney stones, and may accumulate in tissues. Cruciferous vegetables contain goitrogens that impair thyroid function.

**Nightshade "superfoods"** — Goji berries, tomatoes, and peppers contain lectins and solanine that can damage gut lining and trigger autoimmune flares.

**Plant-based meat alternatives** — Products like Beyond Meat and Impossible Burger are highly processed, typically contain seed oils (canola), and rely on plant protein isolates with defense chemicals. They contradict Saladino's framework at every level.

**Fiber-rich "gut health" foods** — Cereals, granola, and whole grains marketed as essential for gut health actually cause the symptoms they claim to fix. Saladino calls fiber a "fairy tale" and cites a 2012 randomized controlled trial showing 100% resolution of constipation on zero-fiber diets.

**Organic processed foods** — An "organic" label doesn't mean a product is healthy. Many organic packaged foods still contain seed oils, sugar, and processed ingredients. The organic label addresses pesticide use, not nutritional quality.

**Multivitamins** — Poor bioavailability forms, industrial binders (silicon dioxide, polyvinyl pyrrolidone), and completely unnecessary if eating organ meats. Saladino calls liver "nature's multivitamin."

**Pasteurized/filtered honey** — Processing destroys beneficial enzymes, antioxidants, and minerals. Buy raw, unfiltered honey instead — his recommended brand is Lineage Provisions.

**American cheese** — The FDA doesn't classify it as real cheese (less than 50% actual cheese content). Also contains microplastics.

**Alkaline water** — Saladino considers artificially alkalinized water unnecessary and potentially harmful to stomach acid. He recommends mineral-rich spring water instead (Gerolsteiner, Topo Chico).

**Restaurant food marketed as healthy** — Chipotle cooks with rice bran oil. In-N-Out fries are cooked in cottonseed oil blend. Even "farm-to-table" and fine dining restaurants typically use canola or grapeseed oil.

## Key Points

- "Natural" and "organic" labels on packaged foods do not mean the product is free of seed oils or plant toxins
- Keto processed foods often contain seed oils — the marketing hides the worst ingredient behind low-carb counts
- Conventional chicken is one of the highest sources of dietary linoleic acid due to corn/soy feeding
- Commercial fish oil is typically oxidized before purchase and may be pro-inflammatory
- Turmeric is high in oxalates — a plant toxin, not an anti-inflammatory remedy
- Plant-based meat alternatives are highly processed and contain seed oils
- Store-bought pasteurized yogurt is a poor probiotic compared to raw kefir
- The simplest filter: if it has a nutrition label and health claims, read the ingredients for seed oils before trusting the marketing

## Common Mistakes

- **Trusting front-of-package health claims** — "heart-healthy," "natural," "organic," and "high-protein" labels say nothing about whether the product contains seed oils
- **Thinking organic = healthy** — organic packaged foods routinely contain organic canola oil, organic cane sugar, and other processed ingredients
- **Replacing beef with chicken for health** — this is one of the most common and counterproductive dietary swaps
- **Taking fish oil for omega-3s** — eat wild-caught fatty fish, salmon roe, or grass-fed beef instead
- **Buying flavored yogurt as a healthy snack** — it's pasteurized dairy with added sugar and artificial flavors; raw kefir is the real probiotic food
- **Assuming restaurant "healthy options" are actually healthy** — grilled chicken at a restaurant is still cooked in seed oil unless you specifically ask
