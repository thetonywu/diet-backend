# How to Reduce Inflammation on the Animal-Based Diet

**Categories:** Chronic Disease, Seed Oils, Gut Health

## TLDR

Paul Saladino identifies three root causes of chronic inflammation: seed oils (linoleic acid damaging mitochondria and cell membranes), plant defense chemicals (lectins, oxalates, and gluten damaging the gut lining), and gut permeability allowing bacterial endotoxin into the bloodstream. His protocol eliminates these triggers, restores nutrient density through organ meats, and tracks hsCRP and fasting insulin as primary inflammation markers.

## Background

Chronic inflammation underlies most modern disease — heart disease, autoimmune conditions, metabolic syndrome, skin disorders, and joint pain. Conventional approaches often recommend "anti-inflammatory" foods like turmeric, leafy greens, and fish oil. Saladino argues this is backwards: these foods either contain plant toxins that cause inflammation or, in the case of fish oil, are oxidized and pro-inflammatory. The solution is to remove the inflammatory triggers rather than try to counteract them with supplements.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Remove the three inflammatory triggers

**1. Seed oils (linoleic acid)**
- Linoleic acid accumulates in fat cells, oxidizes into OXLAMs (4-HNE, 13-HODE), and damages mitochondria
- Oxidized linoleic acid in sebum creates inflammatory skin conditions
- Oxidized LDL (primed by linoleic acid) drives vascular inflammation — the actual cause of atherosclerosis
- Eliminating seed oils is "the most important lever to pull"

**2. Plant defense chemicals**
- Lectins (grains, legumes, nightshades) damage the intestinal lining
- Oxalates (spinach, kale, turmeric, beets) bind calcium and accumulate in tissues
- Gluten triggers zonulin release, opening gut tight junctions — in everyone, not just celiacs
- Isothiocyanates (broccoli, cauliflower) impair thyroid function
- Solanine (potatoes, tomatoes, peppers) is neurotoxic and inflammatory

**3. Gut permeability (leaky gut)**
- Plant toxins and seed oils damage intestinal tight junctions
- LPS (bacterial endotoxin) enters circulation through gaps, triggering systemic immune activation
- This is the mechanistic link between diet and autoimmune conditions (eczema, psoriasis, joint pain)

### Anti-inflammatory diet

- Grass-fed ruminant meat (~1 lb per 100 lbs body weight daily)
- Organ meats: liver (retinol, zinc, B12), heart (CoQ10), spleen (heme iron)
- Low-toxin fruit (watermelon, blueberries, papaya, citrus) for carbohydrates
- Raw honey for carbs and antioxidant status — OJ is also cited positively for reducing lipid peroxidation
- Raw dairy if tolerated (raw kefir for microbiome support)
- Bone broth (1 cup/day) — glycine supports gut repair
- Cook with tallow, butter, or ghee only

### Blood markers to track

| Marker | Target |
|---|---|
| hsCRP | < 1 mg/L |
| Fasting insulin | < 5 μIU/mL |
| Triglycerides | ≤ 70 mg/dL |
| TG/HDL ratio | < 2 |

### Supplements for inflammation

- **Colostrum** (Heart & Soil, 6 caps/day) — immunoglobulins repair gut tight junctions; lactoferrin has anti-inflammatory properties
- **Creatine monohydrate** (5g/day) — reduces inflammation and supports faster recovery
- **Magnesium glycinate or threonate** (200–400mg/day)
- **Heart & Soil Beef Organs** (6 caps/day) — selenium, zinc, CoQ10, K2

## Key Points

- Seed oils are the primary dietary driver of chronic inflammation — linoleic acid's oxidative byproducts damage mitochondria, cell membranes, and vascular endothelium
- Plant polyphenols and antioxidants are reframed as defense chemicals that trigger stress responses, not genuine anti-inflammatory agents
- The body's endogenous antioxidant systems, supported by animal-derived selenium and zinc, are more effective than exogenous plant antioxidants
- Turmeric, a popular "anti-inflammatory" food, is high in oxalates — Saladino considers it harmful
- Fish oil, marketed as anti-inflammatory, is typically oxidized and may be pro-inflammatory
- Autoimmune conditions (eczema, psoriasis, joint pain) are driven by gut permeability from plant toxins and seed oils — they resolve when the dietary triggers are removed
- The omega-6:omega-3 ratio has shifted from an ancestral ~1:1 to 20:1, primarily from seed oils

## Common Mistakes

- **Taking turmeric or curcumin for inflammation** — turmeric is high in oxalates; Saladino considers it a plant toxin, not a remedy
- **Supplementing fish oil for omega-3s** — most products are oxidized; eat grass-fed meat instead
- **Eating "anti-inflammatory" salads** — leafy greens contain oxalates and other defense chemicals that can worsen gut permeability
- **Treating symptoms with NSAIDs while continuing to eat seed oils** — anti-inflammatories don't address the root cause
- **Ignoring gut health** — systemic inflammation often originates from intestinal permeability; healing the gut is foundational
