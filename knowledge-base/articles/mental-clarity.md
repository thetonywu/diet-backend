# How to Improve Mental Clarity, Focus, and Reduce Brain Fog

**Categories:** Nutrients, Supplements, Ancestral Lifestyle

## TLDR

Paul Saladino attributes brain fog to mitochondrial damage from seed oils, nutrient deficiencies (especially creatine, DHA, B12, and choline), insufficient carbohydrates suppressing serotonin, and gut permeability driving systemic inflammation. His fix: eliminate seed oils and plant toxins, eat organ meats (especially brain and liver), take 5g creatine daily for a 5–15% cognitive boost, eat at least 150g carbs from fruit and honey, and replace coffee with raw milk, honey, and creatine.

## Background

Brain fog, poor focus, and mental fatigue are common complaints, especially among people eating the standard American diet or strict low-carb diets. Saladino argues these are not inevitable — they are symptoms of a brain starved of proper fuel and poisoned by mitochondrial toxins. The brain consumes roughly 20% of the body's energy, making it especially vulnerable to mitochondrial dysfunction from seed oils and nutrient deficiencies from a processed-food diet.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

### Remove what impairs cognition

- **Seed oils** — linoleic acid generates oxidative byproducts (4-HNE, 13-HODE) that directly damage brain mitochondria, reducing ATP production
- **Plant defense chemicals** — solanine and chaconine from nightshades (potatoes, tomatoes, peppers) are neurotoxic; Saladino personally reports brain fog from white potatoes
- **Gut-disrupting foods** — lectins, gluten, and oxalates damage gut lining, allowing LPS (bacterial endotoxin) into circulation, triggering neuroinflammation
- **Coffee** — he does not drink coffee and does not recommend it; his morning protocol instead: raw milk + raw honey + 5g creatine + sea salt

### Brain-supporting nutrients

| Nutrient | Source | Cognitive role |
|---|---|---|
| Creatine | Supplement (5g/day) + red meat | Frees 50% of methylation capacity for neurotransmitter synthesis; 5–15% improvement in memory and mental clarity |
| DHA | Beef brain, salmon roe, grass-fed beef fat | Primary structural fat in brain cell membranes |
| Phosphatidylserine | Beef brain | Cell membrane integrity, cognitive function |
| B12 | Liver, all animal foods | Nerve function, myelin production |
| Choline | Liver, egg yolks | Neurotransmitter (acetylcholine) precursor |
| CoQ10 | Heart | Mitochondrial energy production |
| Magnesium (threonate) | Supplement | Only form shown to cross the blood-brain barrier; supports memory, focus, neuroprotection |

### Eat enough carbohydrates

- Minimum 150g/day from fruit and honey — insufficient carbs chronically elevate cortisol, suppress serotonin, and impair sleep, all of which degrade cognitive function
- Serotonin production requires insulin signaling, which requires carbohydrates; serotonin converts to melatonin for restorative sleep
- Saladino experienced insomnia and brain fog on strict zero-carb carnivore, resolving after adding ~300g/day of fruit and honey

### Supplements for cognition

- **Creatine monohydrate** (Lineage Provisions, 5g/day) — effects noticeable within the first week; a single large dose can improve brain function even when sleep-deprived
- **Heart & Soil Mood, Memory & Brain** (6 caps/day) — contains brain, bone marrow, and liver providing DHA, phosphatidylserine, BDNF, and glycine
- **Magnesium L-threonate** (200–400mg/day) — crosses the blood-brain barrier for memory and focus
- **Heart & Soil Beef Organs** (6 caps/day) — B12, CoQ10, heme iron, zinc, selenium

### Support with sleep and light

- Morning sunlight to set circadian rhythm and prime melatonin
- Block blue light 1–2 hours before bed
- Sleep 7–8 hours in complete darkness at consistent times
- Glycine from bone broth promotes deep sleep

## Key Points

- Seed oils damage brain mitochondria, directly reducing the brain's energy supply — eliminating them is the highest-impact intervention for cognitive clarity
- Creatine is one of the most evidence-backed cognitive supplements: it frees methylation capacity for neurotransmitter synthesis and shows 5–15% improvement in memory tasks
- Nightshade alkaloids (solanine, chaconine) are documented neurotoxins that cause brain fog — Saladino experienced this personally with white potatoes
- The gut-brain axis is real: leaky gut from plant toxins allows bacterial endotoxin into circulation, driving neuroinflammation
- Insufficient carbohydrates suppress serotonin production, impairing sleep quality and next-day cognitive function
- DHA from beef brain and salmon roe is the primary structural fat in brain cell membranes — it cannot be adequately obtained from plant sources

## Common Mistakes

- **Using nootropics or methylene blue instead of fixing diet** — methylene blue decreases ATP in healthy mitochondria; fix root causes first
- **Going strict low-carb and experiencing brain fog** — this is carbohydrate deficiency, not detox; add fruit and honey
- **Relying on coffee for focus** — masks the underlying cause; nutrient density and circadian alignment produce sustained clarity
- **Taking fish oil for "brain-healthy omega-3s"** — most products are oxidized; eat beef brain or salmon roe instead
- **Ignoring electrolytes** — sodium depletion causes brain fog, headaches, and fatigue on whole-food diets
- **Eating nightshades without connecting them to brain fog** — potatoes, tomatoes, and peppers contain neurotoxic alkaloids
