# What Does a Typical Day of Eating Look Like on the Animal-Based Diet?

**Categories:** Animal-Based Diet Basics, Recipes & Meal Ideas

## TLDR

A typical animal-based day centers on 2–3 meals eaten within roughly a 10-hour window, built around grass-fed meat and organs, fresh fruit, raw dairy, and honey. Paul Saladino eats no vegetables, no seed oils, and no processed food — and skips coffee entirely. Meals are simple, satisfying, and rich in animal fat and protein with carbs coming from whole fruit and honey.

## Background

One of the most common questions from people curious about the animal-based diet is simply: what do you actually eat? The food list (meat, organs, fruit, honey, raw dairy) can feel unfamiliar, and it's hard to picture what that looks like as real meals across a real day. Paul Saladino shares his own meals in detail — including specific cuts, preparation methods, and timing — making it easy to model your eating on his approach.

## What Paul Saladino Recommends

Saladino structures his eating around **2–3 meals across a roughly 10-hour window**, finishing his last meal 2–3 hours before bed to support his circadian rhythm. He does not do strict intermittent fasting or OMAD, and has stated that one meal a day can suppress testosterone and IGF-1 in active people.

### Pre-Activity Mini Meal (before 6:00 AM)
- Raw goat's or cow's milk
- Glyphosate-free honey
- 5g creatine
- Optional: fresh watermelon juice

### Breakfast / Post-Activity Meal (~8:00–10:00 AM)
- Scrambled eggs — whites slow-cooked by pulling the pan on and off heat, raw yolks folded in at the very end to preserve nutrients; or 6 raw egg yolks on their own
- Avocado
- Mango, papaya, or other ripe tropical fruit
- Fresh watermelon juice or raw kefir

### Lunch (~Noon–1:00 PM)
- Grass-fed, grass-finished skirt steak or ribeye
- ~100g Parmigiano-Reggiano or other raw-milk cheese
- Fresh-squeezed orange juice
- Ripe fruit in season: cucumber (no skin), rambutan, dragon fruit, or papaya
- Seasoned with Jacobsen or Redmond sea salt (low heavy-metal content)

### Dinner (~4:30–5:00 PM)
- Grass-fed 80/20 ground beef, formed into burgers on a grill
- Raw liver (~0.5 oz/day) — frozen at least 2 weeks first for safety, then thawed and eaten raw or lightly cooked
- Other organs (2–4 oz combined): heart, kidney, testicle — or Heart & Soil desiccated organ supplements as a substitute
- Ripe fruit: pineapple, mango, banana, blueberries, or berries
- Raw milk with honey
- Raw butter

### What He Drinks
- Raw goat's or cow's milk (~1 liter/day split across meals)
- Coconut water
- Fresh-squeezed watermelon and orange juice
- Sparkling mineral water — specifically **Gerolsteiner** or **Topo Chico**
- Filtered still water
- **No coffee, no alcohol**

### Daily Targets (at ~170 lbs body weight)
- Meat: ~1.25–1.75 lbs/day
- Organs: ~0.5 oz liver daily + variety of other organs weekly; or Heart & Soil Whole Package supplement
- Honey: ~10 tablespoons/day (Lineage Honey is his preferred brand)
- Carbs: ~300g/day from fruit, honey, and coconut water
- Creatine: 5g/day (Lineage brand)

## Key Points

- Saladino eats 2–3 meals in a ~10-hour window — not strict OMAD or 16:8, but a relaxed time-restricted approach
- Every meal includes a protein anchor (steak, ground beef, or eggs) plus fresh ripe fruit
- Organs are eaten daily — liver especially, at ~0.5 oz/day, which he calls "nature's multivitamin"
- Raw dairy (milk, kefir, butter, raw cheese) is a key staple — pasteurized dairy is considered inferior
- He freezes raw liver before eating to eliminate parasite risk, then thaws and eats it raw
- Honey is a significant calorie source (~10 tablespoons/day), and he uses a CGM to confirm it doesn't raise fasting glucose
- Salt brand matters — he uses Jacobsen or Redmond for low heavy-metal content
- No coffee, no seed oils, no vegetables, no processed foods
- Cooking methods are minimal — steak grilled or pan-seared, eggs gently scrambled, ground beef grilled as burgers
- Fruit choices follow local availability and seasonality; in Costa Rica he eats rambutan, dragon fruit, and mamón chino; in other climates, berries, citrus, banana, and apple work well

## Common Mistakes

- **Skipping organ meats** — many beginners eat only muscle meat and miss the most nutrient-dense part of the diet; if you can't stomach raw liver, use Heart & Soil supplements
- **Eating fruit that isn't ripe** — unripe fruit has more plant defense compounds; Saladino emphasizes only eating fully ripe, sweet fruit
- **OMAD or aggressive fasting** — Saladino specifically cautions against one meal a day for people who are active, noting it can suppress hormones; aim for 2 meals minimum
- **Eating eggs wrong** — cooking egg yolks over high heat destroys heat-sensitive nutrients; fold raw yolks into scrambled whites off-heat at the end, or consume them raw
- **Pasteurized dairy** — he considers pasteurization to degrade nutrients and favor a less healthy milk fat profile; raw dairy is the goal when legally accessible
- **Not finishing eating early enough** — eating right before bed disrupts sleep and circadian rhythms; he finishes his last meal at least 2–3 hours before sleeping
- **Ignoring salt quality** — conventional iodized table salt may contain anti-caking agents and heavy metals; he recommends Jacobsen or Redmond brand sea salt
